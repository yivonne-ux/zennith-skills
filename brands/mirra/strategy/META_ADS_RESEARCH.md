# TASK 1: Top 10 Highest-Converting Meta Ad Formats

## Meta-Level Context (2025–2026)

Before the 10 formats: understand the environment these ads run in.

**Andromeda algorithm shift (2025):** Meta has shifted from niche interest targeting to creative diversification as the primary performance lever. The algorithm now matches creative content to users — meaning your ad creative IS your targeting. Broad audiences + high creative diversity beats narrow targeting + few creatives every time.

**Format performance hierarchy (food/health vertical):**
- Short-form Reels (7–15 sec): Highest reach, best TOFU
- Static 4:5 image: Strongest BOFU conversion when retargeting
- Carousel (4–7 cards): Best MOFU storytelling / product range
- UGC-style video: Highest trust signals, outperforms studio 35%+
- Collection ads: Best mobile shopping experience, AOV +35%

**Benchmarks (Food & Beverage vertical, 2025):**
- Average CTR: 0.9%–1.5% (Feed); 1.5%–2.5% (Reels/Stories)
- Average ROAS: 1.56 (industry average); 3.5–6x achievable with strong creative
- Creative fatigue: eCommerce creatives expire in 7–10 days; refresh every 2 weeks minimum
- Hook rate target: 35–40% (3-sec views / impressions)

## Format 01 — The Appetite-First Static Hero

**Funnel Stage:** TOFU / MOFU

**One-line summary:** A single, ultra-high-quality food photograph that stops the scroll before any copy is read. The food IS the hook.

**Visual Anatomy:**
```
TOP 30%:   Macro food photography — overhead or 45° angle. Plate fills 60–70% of frame.
           Color-saturated, high contrast, steam or garnish for movement.
           Zero text in this zone.

MIDDLE 40%: Product name / dish title in bold, clean typography (2–3 words max).
            ONE credential badge: "Under 500 kcal" OR "Nutritionist-approved" OR
            "Delivered to your door". Never all three.

BOTTOM 30%: Brand logo (small, top-right anchor style).
            CTA overlay strip: "Order by 10pm, delivered tomorrow" or equivalent.
            Price anchor: "From RM19" (Mirra context).
```

**Copy Formula:**
```
Headline: [Desire-first statement, no question mark]
          "The lunch that actually keeps you full."
          "490 kcal. Never boring."

Body: [One social proof signal] + [one friction-removal statement]
      "Built with a nutritionist. Delivered chilled to KL & PJ."

CTA button: "See today's menu" / "Order now"
```

**Why It Converts:**
- Food photography triggers involuntary appetite response within 50ms — before the brain processes text
- Single focal point reduces decision fatigue
- Price anchor + credential badge removes the "is this worth it?" objection in one glance
- 4:5 format fills the mobile feed completely

**Dominant Visual Element:** The food. Everything else is supporting cast.

**Brands that use this:** Daily Harvest (seasonal ingredient hero shots), Snap Kitchen (clean overhead plate shots), Territory Foods (single-meal lifestyle plate), Homey Nutrition MY.

**Mirra application:** Use real Mirra bento photos from Variety Dishes Mirra folder. Apply mirra_filter v4 (full 6-step). Run as retargeting refresh every 10–14 days.

## Format 02 — The Macro/Nutrition Overlay Static

**Funnel Stage:** TOFU (health-conscious cold audience)

**One-line summary:** Food photo with nutrition stats overlaid graphically — speaks directly to calorie-counters, macro-trackers, and the "I want to be healthy but don't want to calculate" audience.

**Visual Anatomy:**
```
TOP 25%:   Brand bar — clean white or brand colour. Logo left. Tagline right.
           "Mirra. Eat clean. Actually enjoy it."

MIDDLE 50%: Food photograph (bento box, hero dish). Rich, warm, appetizing.
            OVERLAID: Semi-transparent nutrition card — pill or badge shape.
            Shows: KCAL (large) | PROTEIN | CARBS | FAT (smaller)
            e.g. "420 kcal  |  32g protein  |  41g carbs  |  11g fat"
            Nutrition card sits at bottom edge of food image zone.

BOTTOM 25%: Dish name (bold, readable).
            ONE benefit line: "Nutritionist-designed. Chef-cooked."
            CTA: "View today's menu →"
```

**Copy Formula:**
```
Headline: [Specific number + outcome]
          "420 calories. 32g protein. Zero guilt."
          "This is what 490 kcal actually looks like."

Body: [Reframe the effort]
      "You track macros. We do the cooking. KL/PJ delivery, order by 10pm."

CTA: "See full nutrition info"
```

**Why It Converts:**
- Specific numbers are pattern-interrupts in an emotional feed
- The visual proof that "healthy doesn't mean tiny portions" is immediately credible
- Speaks to a quantified, high-intent audience (weight loss, fitness, office workers)
- Low copy load — numbers do the persuasion

**Dominant Visual Element:** The nutrition badge / macro numbers.

**Brands that use this:** Trifecta Nutrition (macro-first messaging throughout), Snap Kitchen (calorie-forward product cards), Fuel Good MY, multiple Malaysian fitness meal brands.

**Mirra application:** Run as cold audience awareness. Pair with fitness/wellness interest targeting or Advantage+ broad.

## Format 03 — The Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS) Static or Single Video

**Funnel Stage:** TOFU (cold audience who doesn't yet know they need this)

**One-line summary:** Leads with a relatable frustration ("office lunch roulette is exhausting"), agitates it briefly, then drops the solution. Copy-heavy but structured. Works as both static and 15-sec video.

**Visual Anatomy (Static version):**
```
TOP 30%:   Bold headline text on high-contrast background (dark or brand colour).
           THE PAIN: "You order GrabFood. It arrives cold. You're still hungry at 3pm."
           Font: Heavy sans-serif, 2–3 lines max. White text on dark.

MIDDLE 40%: Food photograph — appetizing, warm, reassuring.
            The visual contrast with the painful text above is intentional.
            Optional: Split composition — left side dark + text, right side food photo.

BOTTOM 30%: THE SOLUTION: "Mirra. Ready-to-heat bento. Under 500 kcal. RM19++"
            Social proof line: "Trusted by 2,000+ KL professionals"
            CTA: "Fix your lunch. Order now."
```

**Copy Formula:**
```
Headline (Pain): [Specific, relatable daily frustration]
                 "Tired of 'healthy' lunch options that don't fill you up?"

Body (Agitate): [Make it real — amplify the cost]
                "You're spending RM30–50 on delivery. Still snacking by 2pm. Still
                feeling heavy by 3pm."

Solution: [Product as relief, not pitch]
          "Mirra bentos: nutritionist-designed, chef-cooked, under 500 kcal.
          Delivered chilled. Ready in 3 minutes."

CTA: "Try your first box" / "See this week's menu"
```

**Why It Converts:**
- PAS is the highest-converting direct response copywriting framework across all DTC categories
- Drama setup → resolution creates emotional investment before the product is shown
- Relatable pain = instant relevance = scroll-stop
- Goodfood (Canadian meal kit) used this exact structure with "Hate cooking but love eating?" as their top-earning ad for over a year

**Dominant Visual Element:** The headline text (top) — it hooks; the food (middle) — it resolves.

**Brands that use this:** Goodfood, HelloFresh (EveryPlate sub-brand), Snap Kitchen, Daily Harvest.

**Mirra application:** 3 PAS angle variants for Mirra:
1. Pain: office lunch decision fatigue
2. Pain: diet food = boring / tasteless / unsatisfying
3. Pain: inconsistent macros from regular delivery apps

## Format 04 — The UGC Talking-Head Testimonial Video

**Funnel Stage:** TOFU / MOFU

**One-line summary:** A real customer (or creator-as-customer) films themselves talking about the product in a casual, native-feeling style. The ad doesn't look like an ad. This is the single highest-ROAS format for food subscription brands in 2024–2025.

**Visual Anatomy (9:16 Reel or 4:5 video):**
```
0–3 sec (HOOK):   Creator on camera, phone held at arm's length or propped.
                  Hook line delivered directly to camera, NO brand intro.
                  "I've been eating this every day for 3 weeks—"
                  OR visual hook: hands opening a chilled bento box.

3–8 sec (STORY):  Show the product. B-roll of unboxing, heating, eating.
                  Voiceover OR on-camera narration (not both).
                  ONE specific detail: "Look at the portion size. That's 420 calories."

8–14 sec (PROOF): One specific result or moment of delight.
                  "I've lost 3kg and I'm not even trying."
                  OR "This is my third week and I haven't had a sad desk lunch since."

14–18 sec (CTA):  Price + link on screen. Creator says it naturally.
                  "They deliver to KL and PJ — link's in the ad."
                  Overlay: "From RM19 | mirra.eats"
```

**Copy Formula (caption):**
```
Hook: [First-person statement with incomplete tension]
      "I was skeptical about meal delivery until I tried this one—"

Body: [Result + one specific detail]
      "3 weeks, real food, under 500 kcal every meal. My nutritionist approved it."

CTA: "First box available now. Link in bio / below."
```

**Why It Converts:**
- UGC-style outperforms studio content by 35% on Meta
- UGC ads generate 4x higher engagement than polished brand creative
- 93% of marketers report UGC outperforms traditional branded content
- Feels native to the feed — algorithm rewards lower-friction creative
- Hook rate consistently 35–45% vs. 20–25% for polished video

**Dominant Visual Element:** The person (trust) and the product (appetite appeal).

**Brands that use this:** HelloFresh (their internal "create TikToks, not ads" strategy), Trifecta (athlete/body transformation testimonials), Daily Harvest (customer story series), territory Foods.

**Mirra application:** Even a simple iPhone video from a satisfied Mirra customer is worth more than a polished studio shoot. Brief 3–5 micro-creators or real customers. Script the structure; let the words be natural.

## Format 05 — The Before/After Lifestyle Split

**Funnel Stage:** TOFU / MOFU

**One-line summary:** Side-by-side or sequential visual showing the contrast between "old lunch" (chaotic GrabFood, sad sandwich) and "new lunch" (Mirra bento, beautiful, nutritious). Lifestyle transformation, not weight loss before/after.

**Visual Anatomy (Static 4:5):**
```
TOP 10%:   Thin brand bar. Logo small.

LEFT HALF (BEFORE):
           Slightly desaturated / cool-toned photo.
           Label: "BEFORE" in small caps.
           Image: messy takeaway bag, sad plastic container, empty snack wrappers.
           OR: stressed person at desk with random food.

RIGHT HALF (AFTER):
           Warm-toned, saturated. Mirra aesthetic.
           Label: "AFTER" in small caps, brand colour.
           Image: Beautiful Mirra bento, organised, appealing.
           Person looking calm/satisfied.

BOTTOM 20%: Copy strip:
           "Your lunch, upgraded. From RM19."
           CTA: "Order this week's menu"
```

**Copy Formula:**
```
Headline: [The transformation, stated plainly]
          "The upgrade your lunch hour needed."
          "GrabFood roulette → nutritionist-designed bentos."

Body: [One line of specificity]
      "Under 500 kcal. Delivered chilled. Ready in 3 minutes. KL & PJ."

CTA: "Shop now" / "See today's menu"
```

**Why It Converts:**
- Before/after UGC content drives 60% higher engagement rates (beauty brand transformation data; analogous for food lifestyle)
- The contrast is processed pre-consciously — the brain categorizes "worse" vs. "better" automatically
- No claims need to be made — the visual makes the argument
- Food lifestyle transformation (not weight loss) avoids Meta ad restrictions on before/after body content

**Dominant Visual Element:** The contrast between the two halves — right side wins.

**Brands that use this:** Snap Kitchen, Territory Foods, various US meal prep subscription brands. Common in UK/AU healthy eating brands.

**Mirra application:** Left side: any generic KL office lunch scene (bought stock or phone photo). Right side: Mirra bento from food photo library. Keep the colour treatment intentionally different to sell the contrast.

## Format 06 — The Carousel Story Sequence

**Funnel Stage:** MOFU (warm audience, consideration)

**One-line summary:** 4–7 card carousel that tells a story across swipes. Each card is a chapter. Best used for warmer audiences who have already seen the brand and need more information to convert.

**Visual Anatomy (7-card carousel, 1:1 or 4:5 per card):**
```
CARD 1 (HOOK):   Full-bleed food photo. Text: "This is what 420 kcal looks like."
                 Right-edge visual cue (arrow / partial image bleeding off edge)
                 signals "there's more to swipe."

CARD 2 (PROBLEM): Text-dominant. Brand colour background.
                  "Most healthy meal options in KL are either expensive, tasteless,
                  or both."

CARD 3 (SOLUTION INTRO): Product hero shot — bento box open.
                          "Mirra does both differently."

CARD 4 (PROOF — INGREDIENTS): Ingredient breakdown or nutritionist badge.
                               "Every meal designed by a certified nutritionist."
                               "Under 500 kcal. 25–35g protein."

CARD 5 (SOCIAL PROOF): Customer testimonial — pull quote on background.
                        "[Name], KL professional: 'I've been ordering 3x a week
                         for 2 months. My energy at 3pm is completely different.'"

CARD 6 (RANGE): 3 mini product images — "This week's menu."
                Shows variety. Counters "I'll get bored of it" objection.

CARD 7 (CTA): Brand colour card. Large text:
              "First order from RM19. KL & PJ delivery."
              "Order by 10pm — delivered tomorrow."
              [Shop Now button]
```

**Copy Formula (caption):**
```
Hook: Swipe to see what your lunch could look like →
Body: We built Mirra to solve the KL healthy lunch problem.
      [3–4 lines max. Mirror the card journey.]
CTA: Link below. Order by 10pm tonight.
```

**Why It Converts:**
- Each swipe is a micro-commitment — the more cards viewed, the higher the intent signal
- Carousel view depth is a powerful retargeting signal — create custom audience of "3+ cards viewed"
- Best for answering objections (price, taste, portion size, variety) across multiple cards
- Oats Overnight: 20-flavor carousel became a top-performing creative
- Proven format for consideration stage: 30–40% lower CPA than video for warmer audiences

**Dominant Visual Element:** Card 1 food photo + the swipe momentum.

**Brands that use this:** HelloFresh (menu variety carousels), Daily Harvest (7-card subscription value carousel), Snap Kitchen, Territory Foods.

**Mirra application:** Card 3+ can use cat06-style brand design with Mirra typography. Card 1 MUST be food photography. Build 2 variants: one food-led (Card 1 = food hero), one problem-led (Card 1 = pain text card).

## Format 07 — The Offer / Urgency Static

**Funnel Stage:** BOFU (high-intent retargeting)

**One-line summary:** Direct response ad. The offer is front and center. No storytelling. Urgency and value are the entire message. Works exclusively in retargeting — on cold audiences it feels pushy; on warm audiences it closes.

**Visual Anatomy:**
```
TOP 30%:   Large, bold discount / offer callout.
           "RM5 OFF your first order"
           OR "Free delivery this week only"
           OR "Try 3 bentos for RM49"
           Dark or high-contrast background. Offer in large, impossible-to-miss type.

MIDDLE 40%: Food photograph — best product, most appetizing shot.
            Semi-transparent urgency badge: "Limited slots this week"
            OR countdown-style language: "Order before 10pm tonight"

BOTTOM 30%: Product name + price + key credential.
            "Mirra bento — from RM19. Nutritionist-approved. KL & PJ."
            CTA button: "Claim offer" / "Order now"
```

**Copy Formula:**
```
Headline: [Offer + deadline]
          "RM5 off your first Mirra box — this week only."

Body: [Remove the last friction point]
      "420 kcal. Delivered chilled. Ready in 3 minutes. Order by 10pm,
      delivered tomorrow."

CTA: "Claim offer now"
```

**Why It Converts:**
- In retargeting, the audience already wants the product — the offer removes the "not now" objection
- HelloFresh's countdown-timer offer page increased sales by 332%
- Urgency language increases CTR by 15–25% in retargeting contexts
- Clean, simple layout processes faster — decision has already been made; friction removal = conversion
- Adding "free" or a discount to CTA measurably lifts conversion rates

**Dominant Visual Element:** The offer headline — the number/discount.

**Brands that use this:** Every major DTC food delivery brand. HelloFresh (countdown "Claim Offer"), Daily Harvest ("Save up to $40 on your first box" repeated in headline + body), Blue Apron (free meals first box).

**Mirra application:** Run ONLY to website visitors (7-day), Instagram engagers (30-day), and add-to-cart abandoners. Never run to cold audiences. Refresh offer every 2 weeks to avoid fatigue.

## Format 08 — The Founder/Brand Story Video

**Funnel Stage:** TOFU / MOFU (trust-building, brand affinity)

**One-line summary:** Founder or brand team on camera talking about WHY Mirra exists. Not polished. Not scripted to perfection. Authentic brand voice. This is the format that builds the "I know this brand" feeling that makes all other ads convert better.

**Visual Anatomy (Reel, 9:16, 20–45 sec):**
```
0–3 sec:    Founder/face on camera. Hook is a personal admission or story.
            "I spent three years ordering GrabFood every single day
            in KL. And I was exhausted and sluggish every afternoon."

3–15 sec:   The problem they built for. Show it, don't tell it.
            B-roll: sad office lunch scene, then Mirra kitchen/food prep.
            Founder VO continues.

15–30 sec:  The product. Show the bento box, the process, the nutrition care.
            Founder: "So we built Mirra. Nutritionist-designed. Under 500 kcal.
            Every single meal."

30–45 sec:  Proof + CTA.
            "KL and PJ — order by 10pm, delivered tomorrow. From RM19."
            Text on screen: Website / DM to order.
```

**Copy Formula (caption):**
```
Hook: [Personal and specific]
      "I built Mirra because I couldn't find a single healthy lunch in KL
      that was actually good."

Body: [Why this matters + what they built]
      [2–4 sentences, conversational]

CTA: "Link below. Order this week's menu."
```

**Why It Converts:**
- Founder-led content creates irreplaceable trust in a market full of interchangeable delivery brands
- Interview-style and founder Q&A formats outperform traditional voiceover-led ads in wellness categories
- Narrative content achieves 42% higher engagement than product shots alone
- This is the "halo effect" format — after seeing it, all other Mirra ads feel more trustworthy
- HelloFresh and Daily Harvest both use founder/brand story as a core top-of-funnel format

**Dominant Visual Element:** The founder's face (trust), the food (appetite).

**Brands that use this:** Daily Harvest (Rachel Drori founder story is core to their TOFU), HelloFresh brand story content, Territory Foods "why we cook" content.

**Mirra application:** Shoot once, repurpose extensively. A single 60-sec founder video can be cut into: 15-sec Reel hook, 30-sec MOFU video, static thumbnail pull quote. High leverage for minimal production investment.

## Format 09 — The Social Proof / Review Aggregate Static

**Funnel Stage:** MOFU / BOFU

**One-line summary:** A designed graphic featuring customer reviews, star ratings, and testimonial quotes. The product is secondary — the proof is the message. Often called a "review wall" ad.

**Visual Anatomy:**
```
TOP 20%:   Brand logo + "What KL is saying about Mirra"
           OR "★★★★★ 200+ reviews"
           Warm background (cream/blush — Mirra palette).

MIDDLE 60%: 2–3 testimonial cards (designed, not screenshots — but
            screenshot-style fidelity to feel authentic).

            Each card:
            - Name (first name only) + location (e.g. "Sarah, Bangsar")
            - Star rating (5 stars graphic)
            - 2–3 sentence quote: specific, outcome-focused

            BEST testimonial quotes to feature:
            - Specific weight/energy result
            - Specific portion size surprise ("I was shocked how filling it was")
            - Specific flavour praise
            - Lifestyle fit ("Finally a lunch I look forward to")

BOTTOM 20%: Product + offer.
            "Under 500 kcal. From RM19. KL & PJ delivery."
            CTA: "Join them. Order now."
```

**Copy Formula:**
```
Headline: [Social proof as fact, not boast]
          "200+ KL professionals eat Mirra every week."
          "What changed when they stopped ordering GrabFood."

Body: [Restate the social proof + add specificity]
      "Nutritionist-approved meals, under 500 kcal, delivered chilled.
      They switched. You can too."

CTA: "Start your first order"
```

**Why It Converts:**
- Social proof is the highest-trust signal in digital advertising — 93% of consumers read reviews before purchasing
- Retargeting audiences who have visited the site respond extremely strongly to this format (removes final hesitation)
- Specific testimonials outperform general ("love it!") by 3–4x — specificity = credibility
- "Social proof Instagram ads" see up to 3.2x higher CTR in retargeting vs. generic web visitor ads

**Dominant Visual Element:** The review cards themselves — the words of real customers.

**Brands that use this:** Daily Harvest (review aggregate ads are core MOFU), Snap Kitchen, Trifecta Nutrition (athlete testimonial walls), Territory Foods.

**Mirra application:** Collect 20+ specific testimonials from WhatsApp customers before running this format. Design as Mirra-branded cards (blush/cream palette). Screenshot aesthetic is fine and preferred over overly polished design.

## Format 10 — The Menu / Range Showcase Collection Ad

**Funnel Stage:** MOFU / BOFU (purchase intent)

**One-line summary:** A Collection ad (or carousel) showing multiple menu items in one ad. Functions like a visual menu card. Best for audiences who are interested but haven't decided what to order — showing variety removes the "I'll get bored" or "is there enough choice?" objection.

**Visual Anatomy (Collection Ad format, or 7-card Carousel):**
```
HERO IMAGE (top of Collection):
           Full-bleed bento spread — multiple items visible.
           Text overlay: "This week's Mirra menu"
           Brand logo bottom-right.

GRID TILES (below hero — 4 product tiles):
           Each tile: ONE dish photo.
           Overlay: Dish name + kcal count.
           e.g. "Teriyaki Chicken Bento — 420 kcal"
                "Salmon Brown Rice Bowl — 390 kcal"
                "Miso Glazed Chicken — 445 kcal"
                "Spiced Lamb Wrap — 480 kcal"
```

**Copy Formula:**
```
Headline: [Menu as the offer]
          "This week's Mirra menu — all under 500 kcal."
          "4 new dishes. All nutritionist-approved. All under RM25."

Body: [Specific + permission to choose]
      "Choose your meals. We deliver chilled to KL & PJ.
      Order by 10pm, delivered tomorrow."

CTA: "Shop the menu" / "View all dishes"
```

**Why It Converts:**
- Variety is one of the top objections for meal delivery — this format directly dissolves it
- Collection ads increase AOV by 35% on average (mobile-native shopping experience)
- Menu-style presentation frames the ad as useful content, not an interruption
- Works especially well for weekly repeat customers — menu freshness is a retention driver
- Oats Overnight's 20-flavor carousel drove top creative performance

**Dominant Visual Element:** The variety of food images — the grid itself.

**Brands that use this:** HelloFresh (weekly menu ads are a staple), Daily Harvest (seasonal menu updates), Snap Kitchen (this week's specials format), Territory Foods.

**Mirra application:** Rotate weekly with new menu. This is an always-on format — update the tiles every Monday. Run to website visitors + lookalike of existing customers simultaneously.

**What HelloFresh does best:** Scale — they run 100+ variations simultaneously across 12 markets. Their creative production is automated via Smartly.io, allowing personalized video ads refreshed daily. The key lesson: volume of variation + platform-native format = their competitive moat.

- "As seen on Netflix" credibility ad (leverages documentary placement)
- Reels: meal unboxing + "this is what I eat to [achieve X goal]"

**Headline formula:**
- Performance: "Fuel like an athlete. Eat like a foodie."
- Macro-first: "35g protein. 420 kcal. Delivered."
- Aspiration: "The #1 fitness meal delivery service"
- Social proof: "As seen on Netflix" — credibility anchor

**Visual style:**
- Athletic, high-energy aesthetic — saturated, bold
- Real athletes and real bodies (not models) — authentic
- Macro nutrition data always visible
- Dark backgrounds or natural earth tones (opposite of Daily Harvest's bright palette)
- Organic certification marks visible

**Offer / CTA:**
- 20–30% off first order
- "Start your plan" framing (aligns with fitness goal-setting)
- Keto / Paleo / Clean / Vegan plan selector
- "Build your meal plan"

**What Trifecta does best:** Niche community ownership. They own the "athlete meal prep" identity so completely that their ads look like fitness content, not food delivery ads. The audience self-selects immediately. Their "As seen on Netflix" credibility hook is a master class in leveraging earned media in paid ads.

## 2.6 SE Asia — Grain SG / Nourish SG / Malaysian Comparators

**Note:** SE Asia healthy meal delivery brands have not reached the creative sophistication of US/EU competitors. This is an opportunity, not a constraint.

**Grain SG (grain.com.sg)**

- Ad approach: primarily organic content with occasional promoted posts

- Pricing: from SGD 8.95/meal — premium positioning
- Key insight: under-invests in paid social relative to brand quality

**Nourish SG**
- Salad-focused (not hot meals) — premium organic ingredients
- Very small paid social presence
- Key insight: has not built a performance marketing playbook

**Malaysian Healthy Meal Delivery (Healthymeal2u, Homey, Fuel Good MY, Phew Fit Kitchen):**

These brands share common patterns:
- Instagram-first (not Facebook) with 18K–50K followers
- Mostly organic content, minimal or no paid ads

- Visual style: home-cooked aesthetic, not polished
- Pricing: RM15–RM35/meal range
- Key insight: **none have built a systematic paid Meta ads funnel**. The market is wide open for a brand that shows up with US-quality creative discipline.

**Gap and opportunity for Mirra:**
The Malaysian healthy meal delivery category has NO brand running a full TOFU–MOFU–BOFU Meta ads funnel with high-quality creative. Every competitor is either:
- Not running paid ads at all
- Running organic-only Instagram content
- Running basic boosted posts (not structured campaigns)

Mirra has the opportunity to be the first Malaysian healthy bento brand with a US-quality Meta ads creative system. Given that the Malaysian food delivery market is highly price-aware and WhatsApp-conversion-driven, ads should route to WhatsApp CTA or a simple order page — not a complex e-commerce funnel.

# TASK 3: Meta Campaign Structure — 1-Product Campaign

## Campaign Architecture Overview

```
CAMPAIGN
├── TOFU CAMPAIGN (Awareness/Traffic) — 20–25% of budget
│   ├── Ad Set 1: Broad/Advantage+ (age 22–45, KL/PJ, interest: health, fitness, meal prep)
│   ├── Ad Set 2: Lookalike 1% (based on existing customers / page engagers)
│   └── Creatives: 4–5 ad formats (see below)
│
├── MOFU CAMPAIGN (Engagement/Traffic) — 20–25% of budget
│   ├── Ad Set 1: Instagram engagers (30–90 day)
│   ├── Ad Set 2: Video viewers 50%+ (30 day)
│   ├── Ad Set 3: Website visitors (14 day, excluding purchasers)
│   └── Creatives: 3–4 ad formats (see below)
│
└── BOFU CAMPAIGN (Conversions) — 50–55% of budget
    ├── Ad Set 1: Website visitors (7 day) + Add-to-cart (7 day)
    ├── Ad Set 2: Instagram engagers (7 day) + Profile visitors (7 day)
    ├── Ad Set 3: Existing customer lookalike 2–5%
    └── Creatives: 2–3 ad formats (see below)
```

**Budget split rationale:** New brands without large retargeting pools should weight 30/30/40 (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU). Established brands (6+ months, 5K+ page engagers) can shift to 20/20/60.

## TOFU Creative Types (4–5 creatives per stage)

| # | Format | Why TOFU |
|---|--------|----------|
| T1 | Appetite-First Static Hero (Format 01) | Scroll-stop — no prior brand knowledge needed |
| T2 | UGC Talking-Head Video (Format 04) | Platform-native, highest hook rate for cold audience |
| T3 | Problem-Agitation-Solution Static (Format 03) | Qualifies the right audience instantly |
| T4 | Founder/Brand Story Video (Format 08) | Trust-building for brand-new audiences |
| T5 | Macro/Nutrition Overlay Static (Format 02) | Targets high-intent fitness/health audience |

**TOFU Creative Rules:**
- No offer in TOFU ads (no discount/promo — this is audience building)
- Video length: 7–20 sec max. Hook delivered in first 2 sec.
- Single CTA: "Learn more" or "See menu" — not "Buy now" (premature for cold)
- Run 4:5 for Feed, 9:16 version of same concept for Reels/Stories
- Set UTM parameter: `utm_content=tofu_[creative_id]`

---

## MOFU Creative Types (3–4 creatives per stage)

| # | Format | Why MOFU |
|---|--------|----------|
| M1 | Carousel Story Sequence (Format 06) | Objection-handling across cards, high intent signal per swipe |
| M2 | Social Proof / Review Aggregate (Format 09) | Warm audience needs trust confirmation, not awareness |
| M3 | Menu Range Showcase Carousel (Format 10) | "What's on the menu?" — answers the consideration question |
| M4 | Before/After Lifestyle Split (Format 05) | Aspiration reinforcement for engaged audience |

**MOFU Creative Rules:**
- Can introduce brand credentials ("nutritionist-approved," "under 500 kcal") prominently
- Begin soft offer: "Try your first box" language — not hard discount yet
- Carousel: first card must be a scroll-stop image (food or strong text hook)
- Retarget video viewers with carousel (different format = fresh impression)
- Custom audience: "3+ carousel cards viewed" → move to BOFU immediately

---

## BOFU Creative Types (2–3 creatives per stage)

| # | Format | Why BOFU |
|---|--------|----------|
| B1 | Offer / Urgency Static (Format 07) | Decision already made — friction removal = conversion |
| B2 | Social Proof / Review Aggregate (Format 09) | Final trust signal before purchase |
| B3 | Menu / Range Showcase (Format 10) | "Choose your first order" — makes buying feel easy |

**BOFU Creative Rules:**
- ALWAYS include explicit offer (discount, first-order perk, free delivery)
- CTA: "Order now" / "Claim offer" / "Try this week" — action-forward language
- Urgency language: "Order by 10pm tonight" / "Limited slots this week"
- Keep copy SHORT — this audience knows the brand; remove last objections only
- WhatsApp CTA button if order flow is WhatsApp-based (common in Malaysian market)

## Creative Fatigue Timeline

```
TOFU creatives:    Refresh every 14–21 days (cold audience = slower burn)
MOFU creatives:    Refresh every 10–14 days (warm audience sees more frequently)
BOFU creatives:    Refresh every 7–10 days (hot audience burns fastest — small pool, high frequency)
```

**Fatigue warning signals to monitor:**
1. Frequency > 3.0 for the same creative in any audience
2. CTR drops 20%+ week-over-week
3. CPC increases 30%+ while impression volume holds
4. Meta Ads Manager flags "Creative fatigue" in delivery column

**Response to fatigue:**
- Do NOT pause and restart (wastes learning phase)
- Create new creative under the same ad set
- Retire the fatigued creative after the new one has 7 days of data
- Rotate the fatigued creative back in after 6–8 weeks (audience refreshes)

**Creative refresh calendar for 20-creative Mirra campaign:**
```
Launch week:    All 20 live (TOFU 10 + MOFU 4 + BOFU 3 + 3 test variants)
Week 2–3:       Monitor fatigue signals. Replace worst-performing 20% of BOFU
Week 4:         Refresh 2–3 MOFU creatives. Keep TOFU running.
Week 5–6:       Full BOFU creative refresh. TOFU: retire bottom 2.
Week 7+:        Rolling refresh — always have 2–3 "backup" creatives ready to deploy
```

## Meta Campaign Setup Best Practices (2025)

**Campaign objective:** Use "Sales" (Conversions) objective, not Traffic or Engagement — even for TOFU. Feed the pixel with purchase events from day one.

**Advantage+ Campaign Settings:**
- Enable Advantage+ placements (let Meta serve across FB + IG + Audience Network)
- Enable Advantage+ Creative (allow Meta to crop, adjust brightness, etc.) — BUT upload 4:5 as primary to avoid unwanted crops
- Advantage+ Audience: Use for TOFU. Manual for MOFU/BOFU.

**Pixel + Event Setup (Critical):**
- Fire ViewContent on product/menu page
- Fire AddToCart on cart/order initiation
- Fire InitiateCheckout on checkout start
- Fire Purchase on order confirmation
- For WhatsApp-based orders: fire Lead event on WhatsApp button click; Purchase on manual confirmation

**Budget:**
- Start: RM 50–100/day minimum for learning phase (need 50 conversion events/week)
- Scale: 15–20% daily budget increase max. Never more — resets learning.
- Floor: RM 30/day per ad set minimum to give the algorithm data

**Campaign consolidation (Andromeda 2025 guidance):**
- Fewer campaigns, more creative variation per campaign
- 1–2 campaigns per funnel stage (not 5–10)
- All creative diversity within the same campaign/ad set
- Let the algorithm match creative to sub-audiences internally

## Mirra-Specific Creative Rules

1. **Food photography is the #1 asset.** Every ad that includes real Mirra food will outperform every ad that doesn't. Invest in a half-day food photography shoot if needed.

2. **Under 500 kcal is a power claim.** Use it in every single ad — it is specific, credible, and differentiating in the Malaysian market where competitors don't quantify this.

3. **"Nutritionist-approved" > "healthy."** Specific credentials outperform vague health claims. Use the actual credential.

4. **WhatsApp CTA is likely the highest-converting for Malaysian market.** Route BOFU ads to WhatsApp DM or a single-click order page. Avoid complex e-commerce checkout flows.

5. **Price anchor RM19 is a competitive advantage.** State it prominently — the target audience expects premium healthy food to cost RM30–50. RM19 as an anchor price is a genuine surprise.

6. **KL & PJ delivery specificity = trust.** Generic "delivery available" is weaker than "KL & PJ delivery, order by 10pm, delivered tomorrow." Specificity removes the delivery-viability objection.

7. **Mirra visual identity (blush/dusty rose/crimson/cream) must be consistent across all creatives.** Meta's algorithm favors brand-consistent creatives — they generate higher relevance scores because the audience learns to recognize Mirra-coloured ads.

8. **Run 5–10 creative variants minimum before optimizing.** Campaigns with 5–10 assets outperform single-asset campaigns by 44%. Do not launch with 1–2 creatives.

## Copy Voice for Mirra Ads (Brand Alignment)

Per Mirra brand DNA: girlboss, unapologetic, no exclamation marks, no brand references, viral/sendable.

**What this means for ad copy:**
- Confident, not apologetic: "490 kcal. Real food." not "Only 490 calories!"
- Direct: "Order by 10pm." not "You can place your order before 10pm!"
- Smart: "Your macros. Our cooking." not "We make healthy eating easy for you!"
- No competitor name-drops: "GrabFood roulette" as a concept is fine; naming GrabFood as a company in ads is not recommended
- Specific numbers over vague claims: "420 kcal, 32g protein" > "nutritious and filling"

**Headline test:** Would a KL girlboss screenshot this and send it to her group chat? If yes, the copy is right. If it sounds like a corporate food brand, revise.
