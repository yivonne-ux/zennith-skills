# 3. ANDROMEDA ALGORITHM — HOW ADS ARE SCORED

## How Andromeda Works (2025-2026)

Andromeda is Meta's AI retrieval system that decides which ads are eligible to be shown. It works in REVERSE — instead of starting with advertiser-defined audiences, it:

1. Scans millions of ads in the system
2. Picks a shortlist of ~1,000 candidates for a specific user
3. Evaluates historical engagement, ad copy, creative format, and quality
4. Predicts which users are most likely to engage

## Creative Similarity Score — The Hidden Killer

**When your ads share >60% visual/audio similarity, Andromeda suppresses them.**

- Andromeda uses computer vision + AI audio analysis to evaluate creative
- Each ad gets an Entity ID based on its visual pattern
- **Creative Similarity Score above 60% triggers retrieval suppression** — your ads are treated as redundant "clutter"
- **Target: Diversity Index where active assets share <40% visual/audio features**

## What This Means for Mirra Ad Production

- Launch **10-15 conceptually distinct assets** per Advantage+ campaign
- "Conceptually distinct" = different angles, tones, creative archetypes — NOT just different text on the same background
- A food close-up, a UGC testimonial, a problem-agitate-solve text ad, a before/after, and a lifestyle shot = 5 genuinely distinct assets
- 5 variations of the same pink food photo with different headlines = Andromeda treats this as 1 asset

## Ranking Signals (Prioritized)
1. Creative quality (70-80% of performance)
2. Engagement prediction (CTR, saves, shares)
3. Conversion history
4. Relevance score
5. Budget/bid (least important)

# 4. CREATIVE BEST PRACTICES: 2x vs 5x ROAS

## What Separates a 2x ROAS Ad from a 5x ROAS Ad

### Visual Design Principles

| Element | 2x ROAS (Average) | 5x ROAS (Top Performer) |
|---------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Style | Polished, agency-style | Raw, authentic, UGC-feel |
| First frame | Product shot | Pattern interrupt / curiosity hook |
| Text overlay | Small, decorative | Large, high-contrast, benefit-driven |
| Format | Horizontal or square | Vertical 9:16 or 4:5 |
| Sound | Silent or generic music | Voiceover / real audio |
| Social proof | None | Star ratings, review count, testimonial |
| CTA | Generic "Learn More" | Specific "Order Your First Box" |

### The 5x ROAS Creative Formula

1. **Authenticity over polish** — Organic, unpolished ads outperform agency-style videos by up to 40%. Selfie videos, TikTok-style clips, and casual screen recordings consistently beat glossy commercials.

2. **One core message, communicated instantly** — Users won't pause to decode a complicated value proposition. The most effective ads communicate ONE thing clearly.

3. **Social proof as conversion driver** — Even a simple overlay like "4.8 stars from 2,400 reviews" dramatically improves trust and reduces friction.

4. **Product PNG overlays + bold black-on-white captions + graphic CTAs** — This specific combination on 4:5 vertical video is the highest-converting visual template.

5. **Native-looking content** — Meta's algorithm favors content that looks organic and native to the platform. Highly polished, overly branded ads trigger "ad blindness."

### For Mirra Static Ads Specifically

- **Hero food shot** must be real, never AI-generated (existing rule — confirmed by research)
- **Price anchor visible** — HelloFresh success: concrete numbers ("6 meals, RM19/meal, ready in 20 min")
- **Contrast ratio** — Large text on high-contrast backgrounds (not subtle overlays)
- **Visual hierarchy in 0.5 seconds** — Users scroll 41% faster than 2020. You have 0.5 seconds, not 3.
- **AI background generation** — FULLBEAUTY tested Meta's AI background feature: 45% ROAS lift, 22% higher CVR, 36% CTR improvement

# 5. HOOK STRATEGIES — FIRST 3 SECONDS / FIRST GLANCE

## The 0.5-Second Reality

Users' scroll speed has increased 41% since 2020. You have exactly **0.5 seconds** to capture attention before they move on. 73% of e-commerce video ads fail within the first three seconds because they LOOK like ads.

## Video Hook Types That Convert

### Type 1: The "Investment" Hook
Talk about the time, money, or effort someone wasted before finding your solution.
> "I used to spend RM80/day on grab food that made me feel terrible..."

**Why it works:** Targets people with the same failed attempts. Builds trust through empathy.

### Type 2: The "Give Me [Time]" Hook
> "Give me 30 seconds and I'll show you how to eat clean for RM19 a meal"

**Why it works:** When people mentally agree to give you time, they stick around longer. Boosts "hook rate" and "hold rate" — both signals the algorithm loves.

### Type 3: The Pattern Interrupt
Unexpected visual/audio that breaks the scroll pattern.
> [Sound of a lunch box slamming open] + extreme close-up of food

**Why it works:** Disrupts autopilot scrolling.

### Type 4: The Controversial Statement
> "Meal prep is a waste of time. Here's what smart people do instead."

**Why it works:** Provokes an emotional response (agreement or disagreement) — both keep attention.

## Static Ad First-Glance Rules

Like a magazine cover, your static ad must communicate its core message at a glance:

1. **Headline visible at thumbnail size** — If text isn't readable at 400px wide, it's too small
2. **Single focal point** — One hero image, not a collage
3. **Color contrast against feed** — Instagram feed is mostly white/light; dark or saturated backgrounds stand out
4. **Numbers stop scrolling** — "RM19/meal" or "500 kcal" performs better than "affordable healthy meals"
5. **Face in frame** — Ads with human faces get higher engagement (but UGC faces, not stock photos)

## The 4-Part Hook Framework (Video)

Every hook that converts has 4 simultaneous elements in the first 3 seconds:

1. **Visual hook** — What grabs the eye (motion, color, face)
2. **Text overlay hook** — Words on screen (benefit or curiosity)
3. **Audio hook** — Sound that stops the scroll
4. **Concept hook** — The idea/angle that creates curiosity gap

# 6. COPY FRAMEWORKS THAT DRIVE PURCHASES

## The Hierarchy of Copy Frameworks (by conversion performance)

### Framework 1: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) — Highest Performer
PAS wins most tests because it starts where the customer already is.

**Structure:**
- **Problem:** Name the pain they already feel
- **Agitate:** Make them feel it more intensely
- **Solve:** Present your product as relief

**Mirra Example:**
> Tired of choosing between healthy food and actually eating lunch on time?
> Every day it's the same: greasy nasi lemak from the office canteen, or starving until 3pm because you "forgot" to meal prep again.
> Mirra delivers nutritionist-approved bentos under 500 kcal to your door. RM19. Done.

**Data:** Health brand using PAS saw 78% more engagement in 3 weeks. CalmMind PAS copy achieved 109% higher landing page conversion.

**Use PAS when:** Your audience KNOWS they have a problem but hasn't fixed it yet. (This is Mirra's market — people who know they eat badly but haven't found a solution.)

### Framework 2: AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
**Use when:** Introducing something new to audiences unfamiliar with the problem.

### Framework 3: Benefit-First
> Get clean, nutritionist-approved meals delivered daily — just RM19/meal.

**Use when:** Audience is solution-aware and comparing options. Best for retargeting.

## Copy Length Testing (Critical)

Test ALL of these — performance varies wildly by audience:
- **Super short:** 1-2 lines (best for Reels overlay)
- **Medium:** 3-5 lines (most common winner for feed)
- **Long:** Full paragraph with story (surprisingly effective for cold audiences)
- **Blog-post length:** 200+ words (works for high-consideration purchases)

## First 2 Lines Are Everything

The first 1-2 lines of primary text are the ONLY part visible before "See More." This is your ad within the ad.

**Rule: 125 characters or fewer for the above-fold text.**

## Psychological Triggers That Drive Purchases (Not Just Engagement)

| Trigger | Example | Why It Converts |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| Specific numbers | "RM19/meal, 500 kcal, delivered by 11am" | Concreteness = trust |
| Social proof | "2,400 reviews, 4.8 stars" | Reduces perceived risk |
| Urgency/scarcity | "This week only: first box 50% off" | Forces decision |
| Loss aversion | "Stop spending RM80/day on unhealthy food" | Pain of current state |
| Identity | "For people who take their health seriously but don't have time to cook" | Self-selection |

## CTA Button Strategy

Specificity is key in 2026:
- "Order Now" > "Learn More" (for direct purchase)
- "Get Your First Box" > "Sign Up" (for subscription)
- CTA must leave zero doubt about what happens next

# 9. CREATIVE TESTING FRAMEWORK

## The 3-Phase Testing Cycle

### Phase 1: Concept Validation (Broad Angles)
- Launch 10-15 conceptually distinct assets simultaneously
- Small budget per asset ($5-10/day each)
- Let Meta's algorithm identify promising performers
- **Duration:** 3-5 days
- **Minimum data:** 10,000 impressions per variation before judging

### Phase 2: Element Isolation
- Take winning concepts from Phase 1
- Test specific variables: different hooks, different CTAs, different visuals
- One variable at a time for clean data
- **Duration:** 5-7 days
- **Minimum data:** 50+ conversions for directional signals, 100+ for high confidence

### Phase 3: Scale
- Push budget into proven winners
- Duplicate winning creative into dedicated scaling campaigns
- Develop variations of winning concepts to maintain freshness
- Increase budgets gradually (max 20%/day)
- **Refresh hooks and messaging every 10-14 days**

## Kill Criteria (When to Pause an Ad)

| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| CPA above target | After 10,000 impressions | Pause |
| CTR declining | >20% drop from peak | Refresh creative |
| Frequency | Above 2.5 | Rotate to new creative |
| No conversions | After spending 2x target CPA | Kill immediately |
| ROAS below floor | Below 1.5x after learning phase | Pause and analyze |

## Testing Volume by Spend Level

| Monthly Spend | New Variations/Week | Active Ads |
|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| <$5K | 3-5 | 5-10 |
| $5K-$20K | 5-10 | 10-20 |
| $20K-$50K | 10-15 | 20-40 |
| $50K+ | 15-20 | 40+ |

## What to Test (Priority Order)

1. **Angle/concept** (biggest impact) — Problem-focused vs benefit-focused vs social-proof-focused
2. **Hook** (first 3 seconds / headline) — Different opening lines/visuals
3. **Format** (video vs static vs carousel) — Same message, different format
4. **Copy length** (short vs medium vs long) — Test all four lengths
5. **CTA** (order now vs get started vs claim offer) — Smallest impact but still worth testing

# 10. AD FATIGUE MANAGEMENT AT SCALE

## The Fatigue Timeline

| Timeframe | What Happens |
|-----------|--------------|
| Week 1-2 | Peak performance |
| Week 3-4 | CPMs increase up to 29%, CTR drops 35% |
| Week 4+ | Severe degradation unless refreshed |

## Refresh Cadence by Campaign Type

| Campaign Type | Refresh Frequency |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Cold prospecting | Every 2-3 weeks |
| Retargeting | Every 4-6 weeks |
| High-budget broad | Weekly rotation (multiple creatives simultaneously) |
| Advantage+ | Bi-weekly (minimum 5 new assets per cycle) |

## Fatigue Detection Dashboard

Monitor weekly. Fatigue confirmed when:
- Ad frequency climbs past 3.5 AND
- CTR drops 20%+ from peak AND
- CPA increases 30-50%

Export data weekly to correlate frequency with performance decline.

## Creative Rotation Strategy for 30+ Ads/Day

### The Volume Problem
You cannot push budget into 1-2 ads forever. Scaling spend requires proportionally more creative volume.

### The Solution: Modular Creative System

Build a library of interchangeable components:

1. **5-7 hook variations** (different opening frames/lines)
2. **3-4 body narratives** (different angles/stories)
3. **3-4 CTA endings** (different offers/urgency levels)
4. **Mix-and-match** to generate dozens of unique combinations

### AI-Powered Creative Velocity
- AI creative tools cut turnaround time by 68%
- Reduced fatigue-driven spend waste by 19%
- Use AI for background variations, text overlay variations, format adaptation
- Keep core food photography and brand elements consistent

### The Diversity Rule
For every winning ad, create 3-5 variations that are **genuinely different** (not just different text on same image):
- Same message, different format (static → video → carousel)
- Same product, different angle (close-up → lifestyle → UGC)
- Same offer, different emotional trigger (fear → aspiration → social proof)

# 11. FREQUENCY & BUDGET OPTIMIZATION FOR SUBSCRIPTIONS

## Subscription-Specific Meta Strategy

### LTV-Based Bidding
- Bid based on predicted LTV, not first-order value
- For Mirra at RM19/meal: if average subscriber orders 20 meals/month for 4 months = RM1,520 LTV
- You can afford a much higher CAC than single-purchase ROAS suggests
- Train Meta to find users who will STICK, not just trial users

### LTV:CAC Optimization on Meta
- **Target ratio:** 3:1 or higher
- If LTV = RM1,520, you can afford up to RM500 CAC
- **Send LTV signals back to Meta** via Conversions API for subsequent purchases
- This trains the algorithm to find high-retention users, not just cheap first-order buyers

### Budget Scaling Rules
1. **Start with $50/day minimum** — algorithm needs data
2. **Scale by max 20% per day** — larger jumps reset learning
3. **Monitor frequency at each scale point** — frequency above 2.5 = add more creative before adding more budget
4. **60-70% to ASC, 20-30% manual prospecting, 10-20% retargeting**

### Frequency Caps for Subscription
| Funnel Stage | Max Frequency | Why |
|-------------|---------------|-----|
| Cold prospecting | 2.0 | Fresh audiences, avoid annoyance |
| Warm retargeting | 4.0 | They know you, higher tolerance |
| Cart abandonment | 6.0 | High intent, urgency acceptable |
| Existing customers | 1.5 | Don't waste budget on converted users |

### Creative Rotation for Subscriptions
Update ads every 7-14 days depending on spend. Test 4-8 variations per ad set to minimize repetition. Always have the NEXT batch ready before the current batch fatigues.

# 12. LANDING PAGE / POST-CLICK OPTIMIZATION

## The ROAS Multiplier Most Advertisers Ignore

High-performing landing pages cascade into lower CPC and stronger ROAS because Meta rewards relevance with cheaper traffic and more impressions.

## Conversion Rate Benchmarks

| Industry | Average Landing Page CVR |
|----------|------------------------|
| All industries | 2.35% - 6.2% |
| Restaurants & Food (subscription) | Up to 40% (highest category) |

## The 5 Critical Post-Click Factors

### 1. Message Match (Most Important)
The landing page must deliver EXACTLY what the ad promised. If your ad says "RM19/meal, 500 kcal bentos" and your landing page leads with brand story — you lose the conversion.

**Rule:** Headline of landing page = promise of ad. No exceptions.

### 2. Page Speed
- Pages loading in 2.4 seconds convert nearly 2x better than slower pages
- Every additional second of load time reduces conversions by ~7%
- Mobile-first optimization is non-negotiable

### 3. Personalization
- Personalized landing pages increase conversion rates by over 200%
- Dynamic content that matches the ad variant the user clicked
- Segment-specific landing pages > one generic page

### 4. Social Proof on Landing Page
- Reviews/testimonials visible above the fold
- Specific numbers: "2,400+ customers" not "trusted by many"
- Real customer photos (UGC) > stock photography

### 5. Friction Reduction
- Maximum 3 clicks from ad to completed order
- Pre-filled forms where possible
- Guest checkout option (don't force account creation for first order)
- Multiple payment options (e-wallet critical for Malaysian market)

## Landing Page Architecture for Mirra

```
Ad click → Dedicated LP (not homepage)
  ├── Hero: Food photo + price + "Order Now" (above fold)
  ├── Social proof: Reviews + customer count
  ├── Menu preview: 3-4 meal options with kcal counts
  ├── How it works: 3 steps (choose → we cook → we deliver)
  ├── FAQ: Address top objections
  └── Final CTA: "Get Your First Box" with offer
```
