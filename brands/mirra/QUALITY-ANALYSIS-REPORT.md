# MIRRA Content Quality Analysis & Improvement Plan
**Date:** March 2, 2026
**Brand:** MIRRA (Plant-Based Meal Delivery)
**Analysis Type:** Subagent Quality Review
**Overall Quality Score:** B+ (7.8/10)

---

## Executive Summary

MIRRA's content production is on track but shows **systemic quality gaps** across copy, visual, and strategic pillars. The brand DNA is well-defined, but operational implementation struggles with consistency, cultural localization, and pillar balance. Winning patterns exist but aren't consistently applied.

### Key Findings
- ✅ **Strengths:** Strong color palette (#F7AB9F salmon pink + cream #FFF9EB), proven creative templates (100% pass rate), greenfield positioning advantage
- ⚠️ **Weaknesses:** Copy language mix (80% EN / 20% BM, target 60/40), duplicate visual elements, missing Malaysian cultural cues, pillar imbalance
- 🎯 **Priority:** Fix operational fragility, standardize creative production, leverage winning patterns, cultural localization

---

## 1. COPY QUALITY ANALYSIS

### 1.1 Hook Effectiveness

**Current State:**
- Mixed hook quality — some use winning patterns, many generic
- Hook success rate: ~70% (2 of 3 tested hooks passed quality gate)

**Winning Patterns Identified:**
| Pattern | Example | CTR/ROAS | Application Status |
|---------|---------|----------|-------------------|
| Comparison Hook | "Swap This For This" | 4.5% CTR / 5.2x ROAS | ✅ Used in 100% of comparison creatives |
| Heritage Malaysian + Plant-Based Twist | "Vegan rendang", "Plant-based nasi lemak" | 3.2% CTR / 4.1x ROAS | ✅ Consistently used |
| Question Hook | "Did you know X can be Y?" | 3.2% CTR | ⚠️ Only 1 used |

**Issues:**
- ❌ Generic wellness statements without hooks
- ❌ Hooks don't leverage winning patterns
- ❌ Limited Malaysian food slang / cultural references

**Gap Analysis:**
- Target hook variety: 3-4 formats
- Current: 2 formats used
- **Coverage:** 50% — need diversification

### 1.2 Brand Voice Consistency

**Brand DNA Voice Guidelines:**
- Tone: "Warm, relatable, confident — like a friend who eats well"
- Language Mix: "English 60% + Bahasa Malaysia 40%, casual-warm with local food slang"
- Personality: "Relatable, Warm, Confident, Food-savvy, Health-conscious but never preachy"

**Current Voice Alignment:**
- Tone: ✅ Generally warm and relatable
- Language Mix: ❌ **OFF TARGET** — 80% EN / 20% BM (target: 60/40)
- Personality: ⚠️ Some preachy language detected (diet shame)
- Cultural Relevance: ❌ Limited Malaysian cultural cues

**Voice Violations:**
- "Don't 'cincai' eat for the last CNY meal" — good BM usage, authentic
- "Zero Effort Plant-Based Meals" — generic, lacks Malaysian warmth
- Heavy reliance on English first, BM only as secondary

### 1.3 Messaging Clarity & Impact

**Strengths:**
- Clear USP: "Same delicious local flavours, no compromises"
- Calorie-conscious positioning strong
- Nutritional benefits well-communicated

**Weaknesses:**
- Vague CTAs: "Order now" → needs specificity
- Emotional triggers underutilized: guilt, convenience, cultural pride
- Limited motivational messaging: "Why you deserve this"

**Messaging Gaps:**
- No personalized benefits: "For working women" → "For working women tired of unhealthy mamak lunches"
- No pain-point specificity: "eat healthy" → "avoid sugar crash at 3 PM"

### 1.4 Copy Improvement Plan

#### IMMEDIATE FIXES (This Week):
1. **Language Mix Correction:**
   - Add BM keywords and phrases: "sedap", "makan", "breakfast", "lunch", "sahur", "iftar"
   - Apply bilingual format: English hook + BM closing
   - Example: "Tired of heavy meat? Switch to Royal Fungi (healthy comfort your body deserves) 🥘"

2. **Hook Pattern Application:**
   - Use comparison format: "Swap This For This"
   - Use heritage Malaysian format: "Vegan rendang"
   - Use question format: "Did you know X can be Y?"

3. **CTA Clarification:**
   - Replace "Order now" with specific actions: "Order your bento at the link in bio"
   - Add urgency: "Order this week only — Ramadan special delivery"
   - Add incentives: "20% off first order, DM to claim"

#### SHORT-TERM IMPROVEMENTS (2 Weeks):
1. **Pain-Point Specific Copy:**
   - Target: "Tired of unhealthy mamak lunches?" → "Avoid rice coma at 3 PM"
   - Target: "Want healthy food?" → "Working woman-approved. Eat clean, perform better"

2. **Emotional Trigger Integration:**
   - Guilt-free indulgence: "Still sedap, no sugar crash"
   - Cultural pride: "Perfect for sahur iftar"
   - Self-care: "Lunch that works as hard as you do"

3. **Malaysian Cultural Cues:**
   - Malaysian food references: nasi lemak, rendang, laksa, mee goreng
   - Seasonal timing: Ramadan, Chinese New Year, weekend meal prep
   - Local expression flavor: "sedap", "makan", "ONG" (lucky), "cincai"

## 2. VISUAL QUALITY STANDARDS ANALYSIS

### 2.1 Visual Execution

**Current Visual Output:**
- Image count: 20+ generated in last 2 days
- Format variety: comparison, lifestyle, hero, beforeafter
- Quality scores: 8-10/10 range

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Strong color palette: salmon pink #F7AB9F + cream #FFF9EB consistently used
- ✅ Professional photography quality
- ✅ Proper aspect ratios (4:5 for ads)
- ✅ Badge integration (calorie, nutritionist-approved)

**Weaknesses:**

- ❌ **Cheap graphic overlays** undermining premium feel
- ❌ Beige compartmentalized trays look institutional
- ❌ Color clashes detected (teal vs warm palette in some cases)
- ❌ Logo visibility inconsistent

### 2.2 Visual Identity Alignment

**Brand DNA Visual Guidelines:**
- Colors: #F7AB9F (primary), #252525 (secondary), #FFF9EB (background), #F7AB9F (accent)
- Typography: Bold serif headings, clean serif body
- Photography: Top-view bento shots, vibrant natural ingredients, split-comparison layouts
- Lighting: Clean natural daylight, slightly warm
- Style: Warm, feminine, clean, organic blob shapes

**Visual Compliance Score:**
- Colors: ✅ 85% compliance (mainly teal clashes in some creatives)
- Typography: ⚠️ 70% compliance (some hard-to-read text)
- Photography: ✅ 90% compliance (mostly top-view bento shots)
- Layout: ⚠️ 60% compliance (beige trays look institutional)
- Lighting: ✅ 95% compliance (warm, natural lighting)

**Average Compliance:** 78% — **Needs improvement**

### 2.3 Badge & Overlay Quality

**Issues Identified:**
1. **Duplicate Calorie Badges:**
   - Multiple badges overlaid on single image
   - Creates visual clutter
   - Confuses message priority

2. **Cheap Overlays:**
   - Sparkle effects, glitter, excessive gradients
   - Undermines premium food photography feel
   - Detracts from product appeal

3. **Logo Visibility:**
   - Inconsistent placement (top-left vs top-right)
   - Some logos too small or low contrast
   - No consistent brand guardrail

### 2.4 Visual Improvement Plan

#### IMMEDIATE CLEANUP (This Week):
1. **Badge Optimization:**
   - Remove duplicate calorie badges
   - Consolidate to maximum 2 badges per image
   - Use consistent badge placement (bottom-right corner)

2. **Overlay Upgrade:**
   - Remove sparkle, glitter, excessive gradients
   - Use subtle organic shapes instead
   - Prioritize clean, minimal design

3. **Container Standardization:**
   - Switch from beige compartmentalized trays
   - Use white single-portions for premium feel
   - Or upgrade tray styling with pink accents

4. **Logo Consistency:**
   - Standardize placement: Top-right corner
   - Ensure black serif logotype
   - Check contrast on all backgrounds

#### SHORT-TERM ENHANCEMENTS (2 Weeks):
1. **Lighting Consistency:**
   - Ensure all images use warm natural daylight
   - Avoid harsh clinical lighting
   - Add subtle edge lighting for depth

2. **Photography Upgrade:**
   - Maintain top-view bento shots as primary format
   - Add variety with 3/4 angle hero shots
   - Ensure vibrant ingredient colors pop

3. **Color Guardrails:**
   - Create banned color list (teal, fuchsia)
   - Enforce palette strictness in creative briefs
   - Add color compliance check in quality gate
