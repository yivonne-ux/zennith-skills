## CRITICAL INSIGHT #1: The Audience Is NOT the Elderly

XHS skews 70% female, 18-35 years old. The people searching for knee health and joint supplements on XHS are **adult children (25-40 year old women) searching on behalf of their parents**. Content must speak to the DAUGHTER buying for her parents, not the elderly person directly.

This changes everything about XHS copy and positioning vs IG/Video.

## Format Performance

- **Video = 97% of top-500 viral health posts** (for likes)
- **BUT image-text carousels dominate SAVES** — the most valuable metric for education
- Saves are weighted heavily in XHS algorithm (signal lasting value)
- Image-text = "干货" format — users save as reference, screenshot-share to family WeChat groups
- **WeChat sharing is how health content reaches the elderly** — daughter saves on XHS, shares to family group chat

## Visual Design Rules (Proven)

### Cover (Slide 1) — Determines 80% of performance
- **60pt+ bold title** with pain-point hook
- High-saturation color blocks
- "Pain point + contrast" composition
- Readable in 3:4 thumbnail on feed

### Specs
- **3:4 ratio (1242 x 1660px)** — fills feed card correctly
- 1080 x 1440px minimum acceptable
- High-resolution, clean imagery is baseline

### 4 Dominant Visual Styles

**Style 1: Knowledge Card (知识卡片)** — MOST trending
- Clean bg (white, cream, soft pastel)
- Large bold headline at top
- Structured bullet points or numbered lists
- Icons/small illustrations as visual anchors
- F-pattern reading layout
- Progress indicator across carousel

**Style 2: Non-Face Wellness Plan (不露脸养生计划)**
- Fixed cover template with brand colors
- Small title + intro + citation + methods + closing + fixed footer
- Illustration evolving from flat blocks → line-art → IP mascot
- Extremely low cost, high scalability

**Style 3: Medical Infographic (医学科普信息图)**
- Hand-drawn or kawaii illustration elements
- Soft, approachable character illustrations
- Data visualization with friendly graphics
- Medical topics made non-threatening through cute design

**Style 4: Comparison/Before-After (对比图)**
- Split-screen layouts
- Four-square comparison grids (3x higher CTR)
- Timeline progression visuals

### Color Palettes That Work

| Mood | Colors | Use Case |
|------|--------|----------|
| Healing | Soft mint → forest green | Joint health, wellness |
| Trustworthy | Light sky blue → deep ocean | Medical education |
| Warm neutral | Cream/beige bg + green/teal accents | General health science |
| Clean science | White-green combination | Most common for science education |
| TCM/Traditional | Earth tones, muted golds, sage greens | Herbal/traditional content |

**AVOID**: Aggressive reds, harsh clinical whites — reduce approachability.

## Carousel Structure (Proven Template)

1. **Slide 1 (Cover)**: Bold pain-point headline + eye-catching visual
2. **Slide 2**: Problem identification / "Do you have these symptoms?"
3. **Slides 3-6**: Core educational content, ONE point per slide
4. **Slide 7-8**: Practical action steps / daily recommendations
5. **Final slide**: Summary + save/share CTA + disclaimer

**Rules**:
- Each slide independently screenshot-shareable
- Consistent visual template across all slides
- Progress indicator (e.g., "3/8") increases completion
- One knowledge point per ~300 characters
- "Save this for later" CTA on final slide

## Compliance (STRICT — 2025 enforcement)

### Who Can Post Medical Content
- Only verified 三甲 hospital doctors (主治医师+) get medical credentials
- Unverified users CANNOT post as doctors
- XHS processed 469,000 violating medical notes, banned 1,157 accounts

### Banned Language
- "Treat," "cure," "heal," "prevent" diseases
- Disease-specific terminology implying medical efficacy
- Over-promotion of supplement efficacy
- AI-generated health content without disclosure (new 2025 rule)

### How Top Accounts Stay Compliant
- Frame as "educational purposes only" (仅供参考)
- Add "consult healthcare professional" footer
- Use "wellness tips" and "lifestyle recommendations" language
- Focus on prevention, habits, diet — not treatment
- Standardized disclaimer footer on every slide

## DotDot XHS Positioning

Given these findings, DotDot's XHS strategy should be:

1. **Speak to the daughter, not the grandparent** — "Help your parents' knees" not "Fix your knee pain"
2. **Knowledge card format primary** — designed for saves and WeChat sharing
3. **3:4 ratio (1242x1660px)** — not 4:5 like IG
4. **Healing green + warm cream palette** — matches both brand teal and XHS health aesthetic
5. **Compliance-first** — disclaimer footer on every slide, "educational purposes only" framing
6. **Progress indicators** on every carousel
7. **Each slide independently screenshot-shareable** — daughters will screenshot and send to parents via WeChat
