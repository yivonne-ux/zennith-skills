Danny Tsang was a product designer for 23 years. After a German designer friend introduced him to a collagen supplement that relieved his own hip joint pain, Danny started DotDot to help others regain mobility. His stated mission: "Someone once helped me, now my goal is to help as many people as possible regain their mobility." 14 consecutive years at the Hong Kong Brands and Products Expo. 140,000+ users served. 92%+ satisfaction rate.

## Tone of Voice

### DNA
- **Warm + Authoritative**: Like a knowledgeable friend who genuinely cares, not a clinical doctor
- **Personal**: Danny's story is central — first-person testimonials, real experiences
- **Trustworthy**: German origin = quality assurance. 14 years = longevity. 140K users = social proof.
- **Hopeful**: Focus on recovery and regained mobility, not on pain and suffering
- **Practical**: Actionable exercises, clear product explanations, no jargon

### Voice Rules
| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Use Cantonese colloquialisms for warmth (口語化) | Use clinical/medical jargon without explanation |
| Lead with the pain point, then the solution | Lead with product features |
| Share real user stories and Danny's journey | Use stock testimonials or fake reviews |
| Specific numbers (14年, 13萬+用戶, 92%滿意度) | Vague claims ("many people", "very effective") |
| Mention German origin as trust signal | Overstate medical claims (never say "cure" 治癒) |
| Encourage consulting a doctor when appropriate | Replace medical advice |

### Compliance Gate
- NEVER claim to "cure" (治癒/根治) any condition
- NEVER promise specific medical outcomes
- ALWAYS use "help support" (幫助/支持) language for joint health
- Testimonials need "individual results may vary" (個人效果因人而異) qualifier
- Follow HK Trade Descriptions Ordinance for health supplement advertising
