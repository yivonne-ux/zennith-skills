*Extracted from visual analysis of all existing brand work. 9 March 2026.*

## 1. BRAND IDENTITY

**Full Name:** Bloom & Bare
**Tagline:** "Small Seeds, Big Wonders"
**Descriptor:** Family Play Space
**Parent Company:** Sprout Wise S/B
**Location:** B-3A-2, Persiaran Bukit Jalil, KL
**Audience:** Malaysian families with children aged 2-10, primarily Chinese-Malaysian demographic
**Language:** Bilingual — English + Simplified Chinese (Mandarin)

**What Bloom & Bare is:**
A sensory-rich family play space combining messy creative play (bubble labs, neon paint pouring, fluorescent art), structured workshops (emotional intelligence programmes, animal painting, seasonal crafts), birthday party hosting, and a parent-facing beverage bar. It positions itself as a space where children explore freely through creative mess — not a sterile indoor playground.

**Brand personality in three words:** Playful. Warm. Unstructured.

## 3. TYPOGRAPHY

### Primary Display Font — DX Lactos (Dirtyline Studio)
- **Font:** DX Lactos by Dirtyline Studio
- **Style:** Ultra-bold/black weight display serif with retro-modern personality
- **Characteristics:** Chunky letterforms, tight spacing, decorative ampersand "&" with a swash/loop, slightly condensed, high contrast between thick and thin strokes
- **Usage:** Logo lockup, all primary headlines, event titles, promo headers
- **Color:** Almost exclusively black (#1A1A1A) on light backgrounds
- **Size behavior:** Used LARGE — typically 40-60% of design height for headlines

### Body Font — Mabry Pro
- **Font:** Mabry Pro (by Colophon Foundry)
- **Style:** Clean geometric sans-serif, medium to semibold weight
- **Characteristics:** Rounded terminals, even stroke width, modern and friendly, humanist warmth
- **Usage:** "FAMILY PLAY SPACE" subtitle, body copy, event details, pricing, small text
- **Formatting:** Often ALL CAPS with wide letter-spacing (200-400 tracking) for subtitles; sentence case for body

### Social Media — Flexible Typography
Social media posts are NOT locked to DX Lactos + Mabry Pro. Other fonts may be used to fit the context and content of each post. The brand allows typographic play on social — as long as the overall feeling stays warm, playful, and on-brand.

**Guidelines for social font flexibility:**
- DX Lactos remains the default for headlines, but alternatives are allowed when the post concept calls for it
- Mabry Pro remains default for body, but can be swapped for context
- Avoid fonts that feel corporate, sterile, or overly formal
- Any font used should complement (not fight) the mascot/pastel aesthetic
- The logo itself always uses DX Lactos — never substitute

### Typography Rules
1. Headlines are the display font, always bold, always dominant
2. Body text is always the sans-serif, never competes with headlines
3. Chinese text uses a standard clean sans (likely Noto Sans SC or similar system font)
4. Social posts can break font rules when the content demands it
5. Yellow highlight swoosh placed behind key headline words for emphasis
6. Text is almost always black — colored text is rare (only in badges/labels)
7. Pricing text uses the display font at large size for impact

## 4. MASCOT SYSTEM

### The 6 Characters
The mascots are the brand's most distinctive and consistent visual asset. Each represents a nature/emotion concept:

| # | Name (suggested) | Shape | Color | Expression | Personality |
|---|-----------------|-------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Sunny | 7-pointed star/sun | Yellow | Wide-eyed, big smile | Excited, energetic |
| 2 | Cloudy | Puffy cloud | Sky Blue | Closed eyes, gentle smile | Calm, peaceful |
| 3 | Sprout | 3-petal leaf/sprout | Mint Green | Closed "U" eyes, smile | Content, growing |
| 4 | Heartie | Classic heart | Coral Pink | Closed eyes with lashes, tiny tongue | Sweet, loving |
| 5 | Petal | 5-petal flower | Lavender | Side-glancing eyes, smirk | Cheeky, curious |
| 6 | Tangy | Wavy cloud/blob | Orange | Round glasses, smile | Nerdy, friendly |

### Visual Rules
- **Style:** Flat vector, solid fill, no outlines, no gradients
- **Eyes:** Black dots with white circle + star highlight (open) OR curved "U" shapes (closed)
- **Mouth:** Simple black curved line
- **Scale:** Characters work from favicon (32px) to signage (meters)
- **Grouping:** When shown together, they line up in a horizontal row, overlapping slightly
- **Animation potential:** Simple expressions suggest future animation (eyes blink, bounce)

### Known Issue: Order Inconsistency
The mascot lineup order changes across materials. No canonical order has been established.
Observed variations:
- Star, Heart, Cloud, Orange, Leaf, Flower (opening invite)
- Cloud, Star, Heart, Flower, Leaf, Orange (profile logo)
- Flower, Heart, Cloud, Star, Leaf, Orange (BloomnBare-03 logo)

**Recommendation:** Establish one official order and document it.

## 6. LAYOUT & COMPOSITION PATTERNS

### Social Posts (1080x1350 / 4:5)
- Cream/off-white background dominant
- Logo at top (centered)
- Large headline in display font
- Mascots as footer border OR scattered as accent decorations
- Rounded-corner boxes for content sections (pricing, details)
- Yellow starburst badge for callouts
- Generous whitespace — never cramped

### Stories (1080x1920 / 9:16)
- Same cream background
- Larger type, less content
- Mascots used as accent elements (not full lineup)
- High vertical hierarchy

### Event Posters
- More expressive — use enlarged mascot shapes as background elements
- Wavy/organic shapes in brand colors as background patterns
- Photography placed in organic blob frames
- Bilingual (EN above, CN below)

### Print Collateral
- Clean, minimal, professional
- Logo always present
- Mascot footer as signature element
- Black text on cream/white

### Promotional Posts
- Photography as hero (dark UV/neon images)
- White text overlay on dark photos
- Mascots placed as decorative row over photography
- Peach/salmon badge for promo labels

## 7. PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION

### Hero Photography Style: UV/Neon Sensory Play
The brand's most distinctive photography features:
- **Blacklight/UV lighting** — deep purple ambient, neon paint glowing
- **Subject:** Children's hands, paint-covered objects (bunny figurines), bubble foam
- **Colors in photos:** Neon green, neon pink, neon orange paint on deep purple/blue backgrounds
- **Mood:** Magical, immersive, wonder — the "glow" aesthetic
- **Crop:** Close-up to medium, 4:5 portrait ratio for IG

### BTS / Behind-the-Scenes
- Natural light, warm tone
- Construction/build-out documentation
- Text overlay with brand font + logo watermark
- Storytelling tone ("To us, it's a corner. To them, it's a spaceship...")

### Photo Treatment on Graphics
- Photos placed in **organic blob/wavy frames** with thin colored borders
- Slight rotation (2-5 degrees) for playful energy
- Never full-bleed on graphic posts — always framed

## 8. BRAND VOICE (Visual)

### This Brand Looks Like:
- A warm hug from a cool friend
- A curated children's boutique, not a chain play center
- Scandinavian kids' brands meets Malaysian warmth
- Think: playful BUT intentional, colorful BUT not chaotic

### This Brand Should NEVER Look Like:
- Generic indoor playground (primary colors, clip art, cheap fonts)
- Sterile/clinical children's education center
- Over-designed or complex (keep it simple)
- Dark, moody, or "adult cool" (except in UV photography context)
- Busy/cluttered — whitespace is non-negotiable
- Using stock photography of children (always real, always their space)
- Corporate or formal in any way
- Using gradients, drop shadows, bevels, or 3D effects on graphics

### Voice Cues in Design:
- Warm, not hot. Soft, not weak.
- Playful copy that speaks to parents, not at children
- Bilingual execution is natural, not afterthought
- The mascots ARE the brand — they do the emotional heavy lifting
- Let the photography show the magic; let the graphics stay clean
