## Typography Size = Perceived Value (2026-03-13)

MASSIVE Chinese characters (50-60% of canvas height) make ads look expensive and editorial. Smaller type makes them look like cheap food delivery flyers.

**Why:** User rejected V03 fix4 ('午后限定' at ~30% canvas height) saying "lost its big typo feel, dont look nice nor expensive." But loved fix5 with MASSIVE '不重复' dominating 50%+ of canvas — same content, dramatically different perceived quality.

**How to apply:**
- For type-driven ad concepts: characters MUST span 50-60% of canvas height minimum
- Food/imagery is SECONDARY to the typography — it supports, not competes
- Think billboard/magazine editorial, not restaurant menu
- Vertical stacking (one character per line) amplifies the massive feel
- The type IS the design — everything else is supporting cast
- This applies to Chinese, Japanese, Korean characters especially — their visual weight at large scale is a design asset
