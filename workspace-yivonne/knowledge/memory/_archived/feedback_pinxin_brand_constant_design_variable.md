Ads need VARIATION in design but instant brand recognition without seeing the logo.

**What stays constant (brand):**
- PX Green + Gold + dark warm palette
- Typography craft (serif wordmark, CN display weight, golden ratio)
- Food photography mood (warm directional, matte, heritage)
- Tone (confident, heritage, food-first, never shouting)

**What varies (design):**
- Layout (full-bleed, split, minimal, grid, type-dominant)
- Dish (rotate all 18 frozen products)
- Mood (dark moody, warm lifestyle, bright XHS, editorial minimal)
- Copy angle (heritage, convenience, health, price, social proof)
- Format (feed 4:5, story 9:16, square 1:1)

**Why:** User explicitly said "brand is for all, ads design need to have variation, but you know its from the same brand." Same principle as Bloom & Bare.

**How to apply:** Each batch must have structural diversity (Andromeda 6 dimensions) while maintaining palette/type/photography consistency. The scroll-stop test: cover the logo — can you still tell it's Pinxin?
