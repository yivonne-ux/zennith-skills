**PROBLEM (W3 batch):** Overall color across the batch shifted too warm — reddish, yellowish. Looks like cheap instagram filter, not "quiet luxury dining."

**CORRECT DIRECTION:**
- **Natural daylight** — 5000-5300K, not warm sunset orange
- **Muted, desaturated** — quiet luxury = restrained color, not saturated
- **Cool shadows** — shadows should have blue/grey undertone, not warm brown
- **Sophisticated, exclusive** — think high-end restaurant, not food blog
- **Tactile surfaces** — visible wood grain, ceramic texture, linen weave (not smooth/plastic)
- **Food should look REAL** — as it would on a restaurant table under neutral light

**WRONG:** Oversaturated warm gold everything. Orange cast. Yellow tint. Instagram "foodie" filter.
**RIGHT:** Natural light, muted warmth, cool contrast. Like a RM200/head restaurant menu photography.

**How to apply — add this COLOR BLOCK to ALL Pinxin prompts:**

```
COLOR DIRECTION: Natural daylight 5200K. Muted, sophisticated — NOT warm/reddish/oversaturated.
Cool blue-grey shadows. Visible wood grain and ceramic texture. Quiet luxury restaurant feel.
Food color must be NATURAL — as it looks on a real table, not instagram-filtered.
```

**Why:** NANO defaults to warm/golden because "food photography" training data is mostly warm-toned food blogs. Pinxin brand DNA is "quiet luxury" which is COOLER and more restrained than typical food content.
