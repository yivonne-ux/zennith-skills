Never anchor creative direction on a SINGLE reference (e.g. "Cocorrina style"). Always expand limitlessly — synthesize from the deepest wells:
- Design masters (Vignelli, Müller-Brockmann, Saul Bass, Paul Rand, Paula Scher, Neville Brody)
- Art movements (Art Nouveau, Bauhaus, Renaissance, Ukiyo-e, Islamic geometric art, Art Deco)
- Mathematics (Fibonacci, golden ratio, sacred geometry, fractals)
- Architecture, fashion, film, photography, music
- The user's own creative intelligence data

**Why:** Anchoring on one reference = copying, not creating. The user has massive creative intelligence. Surface-level research is not enough. Deep forensic analysis, layered thinking, fundamental breakdown — always.

**How to apply:** When doing creative direction, go 10 layers deep. Don't stop at "what looks good" — understand WHY it works (mathematics, psychology, history, culture). Then synthesize something that has never existed before.
