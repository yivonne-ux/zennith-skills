## CONTENT PLANNING PRE-FLIGHT — UNIVERSAL

### The Problem This Solves
Default behavior: plan content with only 2 post types (hero + quote). Result: boring, no variety, low engagement, user rejects. This happened with PX — 13 food_hero + 8 illustration = rejected as "no variation."

### Before Planning Any Content Batch

1. **Read the brand's content taxonomy** — every brand should have defined categories. Use ALL categories, not just the easy 2.

2. **Map to funnel** — awareness / consideration / conversion / advocacy. Check the ratio. Typical: 15% / 50% / 30% / 5%.

3. **Rotate personas** — who is each post FOR? Never >2 consecutive posts targeting the same persona.

4. **Grid rhythm** — IG grid visual flow. BOLD → PHOTO → CLEAN or equivalent. Never 3 dark posts in a row.

5. **Format variety** — mix singles, carousels, comics, quotes, promos. Target: 40% carousel, 35% reels/video, 15% stories, 10% single.

6. **High-shareability formats** (proven 2026):
   - Recipe step-by-step carousel (highest save rate)
   - "Send this to..." relatable meme (highest DM send)
   - Myth vs Fact educational carousel (2.3x more saves)
   - Before/after transformation
   - This or That interactive
   - Tier list / ranking (debate-triggering)

7. **Calendar check** — is there a festival? Is it brand-appropriate? (see festival fit rules)

8. **Dish/product rotation** — feature ALL products, not just the hero 2-3.

### Content Variety Minimums (per week of 14 posts)
- At least 5 different content categories
- At least 2 carousels
- At least 2 illustration/quote posts
- At least 1 educational
- At least 1 promo/conversion
- No more than 3 consecutive same-type posts

### DM Sends > Likes
In 2026, Instagram's algorithm weights DM sends as the strongest signal. Content that people FORWARD to friends > content people just like. Plan for shareability (utility + identity + humor), not just aesthetics.
