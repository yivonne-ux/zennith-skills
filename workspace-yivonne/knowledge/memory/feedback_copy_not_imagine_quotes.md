## COPY NOT IMAGINE — ABSOLUTE RULE

**User's exact words:** "we should copy, not imagine our own lines and quotes"

### What this means:
1. NEVER write a quote from imagination for social posts
2. ALWAYS find the exact viral quote from a real IG/Twitter/Pinterest post
3. The quote must have PROVEN engagement (likes, shares, saves)
4. Copy the EXACT text (or adapt minimally for brand voice)
5. The viral ref provides BOTH the format AND the copy
6. My job: change the ART STYLE to Mirra, not the words

### Process:
1. Scrape IG accounts (@mytherapistsays, @betches, @thegoodquote, @werenotreallystrangers, @thewritersfeelings, @girlzzzclub)
2. Read the actual captions/text from the images
3. Find the ones with highest engagement signals
4. Use THAT exact text on the Mirra-branded visual

### Example:
- ❌ I wrote: "PROTECT YOUR PEACE LIKE IT PAYS RENT" (imagined)
- ✓ Should be: "your boss at work will be like 'i know these are stressful times' and be the stress in the times" (actual viral tweet, @neet_sol via @mytherapistsays)

### Also applies to:
- Billboard/marquee text
- Typography quote posts
- Illustration captions
- ANY text that appears on the post

**Why:** Invented quotes have zero engagement proof. Viral quotes have PROVEN they trigger DM shares, saves, and comments. The hook is the content, not the design.

**How to apply:** Before writing ANY post copy, search scraped refs for an actual viral quote that fits the topic. If none found, scrape more. Never default to imagination.
