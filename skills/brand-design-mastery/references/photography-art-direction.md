## 6. PHOTOGRAPHY & ART DIRECTION

### Photography Styles in Branding

| Style | Character | When to Use | Tier |
|-------|-----------|-------------|------|
| **Editorial** | Storytelling, conceptual, artistic | Magazine spreads, brand campaigns, narrative content | Premium/Luxury |
| **Lifestyle** | Products in context, aspirational living | Social media, advertising, DTC brands | Mid-Premium |
| **Product** | Clean, controlled, focus on object | E-commerce, catalogs, packaging | All tiers |
| **Documentary** | Authentic, candid, unposed | Brand story, behind-the-scenes, mission-driven brands | Mid-Premium |
| **Abstract** | Texture, pattern, close-up, conceptual | Background imagery, mood pieces, luxury | Premium |
| **Food** | Styled, lit, composed food imagery | F&B brands, recipe content, menus | All tiers |
| **Portrait** | People-focused, team/customer | About pages, testimonials, team profiles | All tiers |

### Art Direction Fundamentals

**Mood Board Creation:**
- Collect 20-50 reference images before any shoot
- Define: color palette, lighting quality, composition style, emotional tone, props/styling
- Share with photographer, stylist, and client BEFORE the shoot
- Include both "yes" references AND "anti-references" (what you do NOT want)

**Shot List:**
- Pre-plan every shot with description, framing, lighting notes, and purpose
- Include hero shots, detail shots, lifestyle shots, and B-roll
- Plan more shots than you need (shoot ratio of 3:1 minimum)

### Lighting Techniques

| Technique | Setup | Mood | Best For |
|-----------|-------|------|----------|
| **Rembrandt** | Key light 45deg to side, slightly above. Creates triangle of light on shadowed cheek | Dramatic, narrative, dimensional | Portraits, moody brand imagery |
| **Butterfly (Paramount)** | Light directly in front, slightly above. Shadow under nose | Glamorous, symmetrical, emphasizes cheekbones | Beauty, fashion, luxury |
| **Split** | Light directly to one side, half face illuminated | Dramatic, moody, artistic | Musicians, artists, editorial |
| **Natural Window** | Soft diffuse light from large window or softbox | Warm, authentic, approachable | Lifestyle, food, product |
| **Golden Hour** | Low sun (just after sunrise / before sunset) | Warm, romantic, magical | Lifestyle, outdoor, editorial |
| **Hard Light** | Direct, small source. Sharp shadows | Graphic, bold, high-contrast | Fashion editorial, bold campaigns |
| **Flat Light** | Even illumination, minimal shadows | Clean, modern, commercial | Product, e-commerce, UI |

### Composition Frameworks

**Rule of Thirds:**
- Divide frame into 3x3 grid. Place subjects at intersections.
- Creates dynamic, off-center compositions that feel natural.
- When to BREAK: centering creates power and symmetry (useful for luxury, authority).

**Golden Spiral (Fibonacci):**
- Spiral based on golden ratio proportions. Subject at the tightest coil.
- Creates natural, flowing compositions that guide the eye.
- Most effective in organic/natural scenes.

**Leading Lines:**
- Use roads, rivers, fences, architecture to guide the eye toward the subject.
- During golden hour, long shadows create natural leading lines.

**Frame Within a Frame:**
- Place subject within archways, doorways, windows, foliage.
- Adds depth, context, and focus. Creates layers in the composition.
- Curved shapes (arches) draw the eye inward naturally.

**Negative Space:**
- Surround subject with emptiness. Creates breathing room and emphasis.
- Signals luxury and premium positioning. Less = more visual weight on the subject.

### Post-Production Standards

**Color Grading:**
- Must be consistent across all brand imagery (define LUTs or presets)
- Luxury: desaturated, low contrast, muted highlights
- Playful: saturated, warm shadows, lifted blacks
- Clinical/Tech: neutral, precise, natural skin tones

**Retouching Ethics:**
- Remove distractions, not reality
- Skin retouching: texture should remain visible
- Product: clean up defects but don't alter the product itself
- Consistency: same retouching approach across all brand imagery

### Stock vs Custom Photography

| Factor | Stock | Custom |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **Cost** | Low per image | High per shoot |
| **Uniqueness** | Competitors may use same image | Exclusive to your brand |
| **Brand fit** | Generic, requires compromise | Tailored to brand DNA |
| **Speed** | Instant | Requires planning and production time |
| **When to use** | Internal docs, placeholders, low-visibility | Hero images, campaigns, core brand expression |

**Rule**: Any imagery that IS the brand should be custom. Anything that supports operations can be stock.
