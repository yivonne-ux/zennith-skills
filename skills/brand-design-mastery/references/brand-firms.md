## 11. THE GREATEST BRAND IDENTITY FIRMS

### Pentagram

**Founded**: 1972, Notting Hill, London. By Alan Fletcher, Theo Crosby, Colin Forbes, Kenneth Grange, Mervyn Kurlansky.

**Partner Model**: No hierarchy. Partners share income equally and own equal portions. No single creative philosophy — each partner brings their own. This structure attracts world-class individual talent while providing infrastructure.

**Methodology**: Before designing anything, understand how the brand is experienced by customers, where it lives, how it functions, what its future is. They think of brand systems "not in terms of architecture but more like gardens" — laying groundwork that can be constantly reconfigured.

**Notable partners (current/past)**: Paula Scher, Michael Bierut, Eddie Opara, Natasha Jen, Abbott Miller, Marina Willer, Luke Hayman.

**Key work**: Citi, MoMA, Mastercard, MIT Media Lab, New York Public Library, Saks Fifth Avenue, The Public Theater, Verizon, Warner Bros.

**On D&AD's all-time list**: Second most-awarded design outfit (beaten only by Apple).

### Sagmeister & Walsh / &Walsh

**Sagmeister (Stefan)**: Austrian-born. Three core elements: Tactile Typography (physical materials as letterforms), Sabbatical Cycle (closes studio every 7 years for non-commercial research), Functional Beauty (aesthetics improve product performance). "We are just emotional human beings trying to create things for other emotional human beings."

**Walsh (Jessica)**: Left to form &Walsh in 2019. Bold, emotional, provocative. Blends handcraft with digital. Focuses on branding, typography, web design, art installations.

**Method**: Highly personal. Organically blends handcrafted set designs with post-produced computer graphics. Balance analogue and digital.

### Collins

**Founded by Brian Collins**. Operates on a "transformation model" — tailored to each brand's current need for change.

**Philosophy**: "Brand experience design" — not just what a brand looks like, but how it BEHAVES in the world. Strategy and design are inseparable.

**Notable work**: Spotify, Twitch, Dropbox, Target, Oatly.

**Key contribution**: Brought strategy-led design thinking to brand identity. Leo Porto (later PORTO ROCHA) was design director here for 4+ years.

### Wolff Olins

**Founded**: ~60 years ago. Global brand consultancy.

**Philosophy**: "Creating transformative brands that move organizations, people and the world forward." Wrote the rule book for transformational branding, then kept tearing it up.

**Notable work**: Google (original color system), Uber, Grubhub, One Young World, (RED), USA Today.

**Method**: Focus on transformation — brands as catalysts for organizational change, not just visual identities.

### Landor

**Founded**: 1941 by Walter Landor. Pioneered many research, design, and consulting methods now standard in branding.

**Footprint**: 26 offices in 19 countries.

**Notable work**: FedEx, BP, P&G (brand architecture), GE, Singapore Airlines, Barclays.

**Strength**: Corporate identity at scale. Deep research methodology. Global implementation expertise.

### Studio Dumbar

**Founded**: 1977, The Hague, by Gert Dumbar.

**Philosophy**: Experimental, boundary-pushing Dutch design. Fragmented, complex, layered typography. Some projects caused consternation among advocates of ordered aesthetics — that was the point.

**Notable work**: Dutch Railways (NS) identity + yellow trains, Holland Festival, Dutch Police identity, Zeebelt theatre posters.

**Recognition**: Third most-awarded design outfit on D&AD's all-time list (behind Apple and Pentagram).

### Ragged Edge

**Founded**: 2007, London.

**Philosophy**: Work is conceptually driven, highly distinctive, and built to scale. Focused on "challenger brands" — brands that disrupt established categories.

**Notable work**: Tilt, Bulb, Papier, Patch, Starling Bank.

### Koto

**Offices**: Los Angeles, New York, London, Berlin, Sydney.

**Philosophy**: Shape the future with optimism, collaboration, and craft.

**Notable work**: Tripadvisor rebrand, Headspace, Airbnb, Google, Square.

**Education initiative**: "Seasoned" — free educational platform using cooking metaphor to teach branding process.

### PORTO ROCHA

**Founded by Leo Porto** (former Collins design director, also worked at Pentagram, Mother, Chermayeff & Geismar & Haviv).

**Offices**: New York and London.

**Philosophy**: Strategically rigorous, emotionally resonant, beautifully designed. Help brands navigate complex challenges.

### Base Design

**Location**: Brussels, New York, Geneva.

**Philosophy**: Minimal, systematic approach. Let the idea speak through disciplined execution.

**Notable work**: Gagosian Gallery, The High Line, International Center of Photography.

### Manual Creative

**Location**: San Francisco.

**Philosophy**: Clean, modern brand systems. Strategy-led, design-executed.

**Notable work**: Mixpanel, Stripe Atlas, Manual itself as a case study in restrained design.
