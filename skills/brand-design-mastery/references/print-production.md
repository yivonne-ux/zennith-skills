## 9. PRINT & PRODUCTION

### Paper Stocks & Finishes

| Stock | Character | Best For |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Uncoated** | Warm, tactile, absorbs ink. Natural feel | Letterhead, stationery, eco brands, literary |
| **Matte Coated** | Smooth but not shiny. Good color accuracy | Brochures, catalogs, photography with subtlety |
| **Gloss Coated** | Shiny, vivid colors, sharp images | Magazines, promotional materials, product catalogs |
| **Silk/Satin** | Between matte and gloss. Elegant | Premium brochures, annual reports |
| **Textured (Laid, Linen, Felt)** | Tactile patterns, craft feel | Stationery, invitations, artisan brands |
| **Metallic** | Shimmer, pearlescent | Luxury packaging, special editions |
| **Kraft** | Unbleached brown. Raw, eco | Sustainable brands, food packaging, artisan |
| **Cotton** | Soft, thick, luxurious | Business cards, premium stationery |

**Weight guide:**
- 80-100 gsm: Standard office paper, internal documents
- 120-170 gsm: Flyers, brochures, premium letterhead
- 200-300 gsm: Postcards, covers, invitations
- 300-400+ gsm: Business cards, packaging, rigid materials

### Print Finishing Techniques

| Technique | Process | Effect | Cost | Best For |
|-----------|---------|--------|------|----------|
| **Letterpress** | Raised surface transfers ink, creates physical impression | Deep, tactile indentation. Vintage, premium feel | High | Invitations, stationery, luxury packaging |
| **Foil Stamping** | Metal die + heat + pressure bonds colored foil to substrate | Metallic/holographic shine on specific areas | High | Logos on packaging, business cards, covers |
| **Embossing** | Raised areas pressed into paper from behind | 3D texture, tactile quality | Medium-High | Logos, borders, decorative elements |
| **Debossing** | Pressed DOWN into paper surface | Subtle, elegant indentation | Medium-High | Luxury packaging, leather goods, stationery |
| **Spot UV** | Clear glossy coating on specific areas against matte background | Contrast between glossy and matte areas | Medium | Business cards, packaging, promotional |
| **Die Cut** | Sharp metal die cuts custom shapes into paper | Custom shapes, windows, unique forms | Medium | Packaging, unique business cards, mailers |
| **Edge Painting** | Color applied to cut edges of thick paper | Colored edges visible from side | Medium | Premium business cards, invitations |
| **Blind Emboss** | Embossing without ink or foil | Subtle, tactile-only impression | Medium | Luxury stationery, understated branding |

### Color Management for Print

**The Process:**
1. Design in RGB (wider gamut, better for screen work)
2. Convert to CMYK for process printing (note: some colors will shift)
3. For critical brand colors, specify Pantone spot colors
4. Request physical proofs (soft proofing on screen is insufficient for critical work)
5. Use G7 Master Certified Printers for consistent color calibration
6. Keep Pantone Color Bridge Book for verified PMS-to-CMYK conversions

**Spot color vs Process color:**
- Spot: pre-mixed Pantone inks. Exact, consistent, reproducible. Required for brand colors.
- Process: CMYK halftone dots. Variable between printers/runs. Acceptable for photography, not for brand-critical colors.

### Packaging Design Principles

**Structural design**: Box construction, material selection, functionality, opening experience
**Graphic design**: Brand expression on the package surface
**User experience**: Unboxing sequence, protection, sustainability, reusability

**The unboxing moment is a brand touchpoint**: Hermes' orange box, Apple's precision packaging, Tiffany's blue box — the package IS the product experience.
