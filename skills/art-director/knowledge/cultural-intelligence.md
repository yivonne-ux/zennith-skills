## CHINA
**Lucky:** Red, gold, 8, 9, 6, dragon, phoenix, lotus, fish
**Unlucky:** White (mourning), black, 4 (death), 7 (ghosts), green hats, clocks as gifts, pears (separation)
**Rules:** Red + gold always safe. Never all-white packaging. Avoid number 4 everywhere.

## JAPAN
**Lucky:** Red, pink (cherry blossom), blue, gold, 7
**Unlucky:** 4 (death), 9 (suffering), 42 (to die), 49 (pain→death), white chrysanthemum (funeral), red ink for names
**Rules:** Dense layouts accepted. ○ = correct (not ✓). Never write names in red.

## SOUTH KOREA
**Lucky:** Obangsaek (5 colors: blue/red/yellow/white/black), yellow (royalty), pink (trust)
**Unlucky:** 4 (death), red ink for names, white (mourning), red gift wrapping, crow
**Rules:** K-beauty/K-pop influences design expectations. NEVER write names in red.

## MALAYSIA
**Lucky:** Green (Islam), yellow (royalty), red (celebration), batik patterns
**Sensitive:** Yellow clothing (political protest), pig/dog imagery, left hand, soles of feet
**Rules:** Multi-ethnic (Malay/Chinese/Indian). Halal essential. Modest imagery.

## INDONESIA
**Lucky:** Red + white (national), batik
**Sensitive:** Green (complex: forbidden in Javanese, sacred in Islam), pig, left hand
**Rules:** World's largest Muslim country. Green requires careful audience research.

## THAILAND
**Lucky:** Yellow (monarchy/Monday), blue (Queen/Friday), pink (King's lucky color), gold (Buddhism)
**Sensitive:** Royal imagery (lese-majeste laws), stepping on money, head touching, Buddha for commerce
**Rules:** Daily color system is followed. Monarchy is sacred. Never use Buddha commercially.

## MIDDLE EAST
**Sacred:** Green (Islam), blue (protection), white (purity), gold (prestige), calligraphy, geometric patterns
**Offensive:** Pig/pork, alcohol, soles of feet, left hand, orange (mourning), casual human figures in religious context
**Rules:** RTL layouts. Ramadan sensitivity. Modesty essential. Calligraphy > illustration. Gold + emerald + deep blue = premium.

## INDIA
**Auspicious:** Red (marriage/prosperity), saffron/orange (spirituality), yellow/turmeric (purification), lotus, elephant (Ganesha)
**Inauspicious:** White (mourning/widowhood), black (inauspicious), cow harm, shoes near sacred spaces
**Rules:** Red = universally positive (opposite of Western "danger"). White = mourning. 22 official languages. Vegetarian green/brown dot system required on food packaging.

## LATIN AMERICA
**Positive:** Vibrant bold colors, red (passion), religious imagery (respectful), Day of Dead motifs (Mexico)
**Sensitive:** Dull/muddy yellow (death in Mexico/Brazil), purple (mourning in Brazil), 13 (unlucky but Tuesday not Friday)
**Rules:** Embrace bold color. Muted palettes feel foreign. Brazil = Portuguese not Spanish. Regional pride matters.

## EUROPE
**Key:** Purple = mourning (Italy). Orange = political (Northern Ireland) vs national pride (Netherlands). Red can = communism (Eastern Europe).
**Rules:** Design-literate consumers. Sustainability expected. GDPR compliance. Regional pride > pan-European. Number 17 unlucky in Italy.

## NORTH AMERICA
**Standard:** Blue = trust. Red = excitement/urgency. Green = nature/money. White = purity. Black = sophistication.
**Sensitive:** Red/blue = partisan (Republican/Democrat). Flag imagery can polarize.
**Rules:** Diverse multicultural. Accessibility enforced. Spanish-language increasingly important (62M+ Hispanic US population).

## UNIVERSAL CHECKLIST (before any international launch)
- [ ] Color meanings checked for target culture
- [ ] Number associations verified (pricing, product counts)
- [ ] Animal imagery appropriate (pigs, dogs, cows, owls)
- [ ] Hand gestures / body language in imagery verified
- [ ] Brand name + tagline translation checked for unintended meanings
- [ ] Religious/spiritual symbols used with cultural consultants
- [ ] Left/right hand conventions confirmed
- [ ] Food/dietary imagery compliant (halal, kosher, veg markers)
- [ ] Tested with native speakers and cultural insiders
- [ ] Historical/political sensitivities reviewed
