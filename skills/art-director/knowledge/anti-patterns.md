# Design Anti-Patterns — 63 Mistakes to Never Make

> Quick-reference checklist. Full explanations in research archive.

## LOGO (AP-01 to AP-08)
1. Generic abstract swoosh — communicates nothing
2. Overcomplicated mark — fails at favicon size
3. Trend-chasing — logos need 10-20 year lifespan, trends last 2-3
4. Competitor mimicry — legal liability + no differentiation
5. Forced negative space — primary shape suffers
6. Illegible wordmark — readability sacrificed for "creativity"
7. Clip art assembly — not ownable, not trademarkable
8. Kitchen sink tagline — taglines change, logos shouldn't

## TYPOGRAPHY (AP-09 to AP-17)
9. Font vomit — max 2-3 typefaces per system
10. Poor kerning — "CLICK" reading as "CL ICK"
11. Decorative font for body — unreadable at paragraph length
12. Fake small caps — faux looks anemic
13. Centered body text — ragged both sides, hard to track
14. Stretching/squishing type — destroys letterforms
15. No hierarchy — all text same size/weight
16. All caps everything — reduces reading speed 13-20%
17. Too-similar font pairing — looks like a mistake

## COLOR (AP-18 to AP-23)
18. Too many colors — 60-30-10 rule (max 3-5 total)
19. Pure black on pure white only — causes eye fatigue
20. Clashing complementary at full saturation — optical vibration
21. Neon overload — dates within 2-3 years
22. Ignoring color blindness — 8% of men affected
23. Violating category color expectations — fighting deep associations

## LAYOUT (AP-24 to AP-29)
24. Fear of white space — luxury = breathing room
25. Decorative borders/dividers — add noise, not information
26. Misaligned elements — subconscious unease
27. Centered everything — passive, static, no hierarchy
28. Text over busy images — illegible
29. No visual entry point — eye wanders randomly

## BRAND STRATEGY (AP-30 to AP-34)
30. Branding as decoration — pretty but no strategy underneath
31. Founder taste over market research — you are not your customer
32. Inconsistency across touchpoints — trust erosion up to 67%
33. Rebranding without reason — Tropicana lost $30M
34. Generic brand voice — if competitor's name fits, start over

## AI-GENERATED (AP-35 to AP-41)
35. AI gradient blob — every SaaS landing page 2024-2026
36. AI-perfect symmetry — uncanny valley, lacks warmth
37. AI text garbling — NEVER let AI generate text
38. AI stock humans — wrong hands, blended teeth, no pores
39. AI lookalike logos — not ownable, recognizably generated
40. Over-reliance on AI moodboards — can't build brand on unowned images
41. Multi-pass AI degradation — compounds errors, never self-corrects

## LUXURY/PREMIUM (AP-42 to AP-45)
42. Lazy minimalism — simplicity without craft feels empty
43. Gold foil everything — overuse cheapens the effect
44. Discount visuals on premium brands — permanently erodes positioning
45. Didot/Bodoni as default luxury — overused, diluted association

## WELLNESS/SPIRITUAL (AP-46 to AP-49)
46. Sage-and-crystal starter pack — every Canva wellness brand
47. Unsubstantiated claims — legally dangerous
48. Appropriated sacred symbols — cultural backlash
49. Exclusively feminine aesthetic for "everyone" — excludes 50%+

## SOCIAL MEDIA (AP-50 to AP-54)
50. Desktop-designed mobile content — 96% users on mobile
51. Inconsistent feed aesthetic — trust signal destroyed
52. Text-heavy social cards — scanning medium, max 15-20 words
53. Ignoring platform safe zones — critical info cropped
54. Generic stock photography — "stock photo blindness"

## PACKAGING/PHYSICAL (AP-55 to AP-56)
55. Misleading visual hierarchy — can't identify product on shelf
56. Print-unaware digital design — neon colors can't reproduce CMYK

## GENERAL (AP-57 to AP-63)
57. Mystery navigation — hidden = doesn't exist
58. Dark patterns — short-term gains, permanent brand damage
59. Designing for awards, not audiences — clever ≠ effective
60. Solving wrong problem beautifully — design can't fix strategy
61. Ignoring context of use — view billboard from moving car
62. The "make it pop" trap — add effects vs fix hierarchy
63. Skipping the brief — 30% understanding before touching tools

## RED FLAGS CHECKLIST
- [ ] Can you read all text at arm's length on a phone?
- [ ] Does it work in grayscale?
- [ ] Can you identify the brand in 2 seconds?
- [ ] Clear 1-2-3 reading order?
- [ ] Tested on actual platform/medium?
- [ ] Fewer than 3 fonts? Fewer than 5 colors?
- [ ] Works at smallest required size?
- [ ] Color-blindness checked?
- [ ] All text rendered by design tools (not AI)?
- [ ] Could this belong to a competitor?
- [ ] Will this look current in 3 years?
- [ ] Someone outside the team validated it?
