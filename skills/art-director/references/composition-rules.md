# Composition Rules — Typography, Color, Materials & Motion

## THE 12 LAWS OF TRANSCENDENT BRANDS

1. **Total commitment > direction.** Charlotte Tilbury (maximalist) and The Row (zero logos) are equally transcendent. Half-measures are forgettable.
2. **Own ONE thing completely.** Glossier = pink. Supreme = red box. Bottega = weave. Not five things. ONE.
3. **Constraint creates identity.** Oatly had no ad budget --> packaging became media. Kinfolk uses 2 text sizes.
4. **The product IS the brand.** Apple hardware = identity. Bottega weave = logo. When inseparable, identity is unkillable.
5. **Absence > presence.** The Row: no logos. Bottega: deleted social media. Aman: no visible branding.
6. **Typography is the most powerful brand tool.** Baron reshaped luxury with Didot + white space. Abloh built an empire with Helvetica + quotation marks.
7. **The unboxing IS the marketing.** Apple: engineered lid speed (3-4 seconds). Byredo: deliberately heavy bottles.
8. **Anti-branding is the strongest branding.** Margiela's removable stitches = most recognized detail. Patagonia "Don't Buy" = 30% growth.
9. **Cultural participation > advertising.** Loewe: meme to product in days. Erewhon: Balenciaga carried their bags on the runway.
10. **The brand must cost you something.** Patagonia refused profitable programs. Bottega deleted millions of followers.
11. **Design every touchpoint.** Stripe: API docs as beautiful as homepage. The gap between "important" and "unimportant" touchpoints reveals mediocrity.
12. **One person's taste > committee's consensus.** Jacquemus art-directs everything personally.

## THE 10 PRINCIPLES OF ENDURING MARKS

1. **Geometric foundation, optical finish.** Math provides structure; the eye provides truth.
2. **Encode multiple levels.** Literal + metaphorical + structural.
3. **Constraint is freedom.** 32 pixels. One brushstroke. Tightest constraints = most universal symbols.
4. **The gap is the meaning.** Enso's opening. Yin-yang's dots. Apple's bite.
5. **Survive the worst medium.** Carved in stone, stamped in wax, 16x16 pixels.
6. **Dynamic balance over static symmetry.** S-curves, tapered swooshes, rotational forms.
7. **One solution, total conviction.** Cassandre presented ONE YSL. Rand demanded his yellow.
8. **Time is the only test.** Chanel CC: 100+ years. Yin-yang: 3,000+ years.
9. **The best marks become invisible.** Nike swoosh no longer needs "Nike."
10. **Intuition first, geometry second.** Janoff drew apples freehand. Golden ratio refined AFTER.

## TYPOGRAPHY MASTERY

### Why Certain Typefaces Feel Beautiful
- **Garamond (1530):** Cut in metal -- physical constraints forced inevitable forms. 500 years.
- **Bodoni (1798):** Extreme thick/thin contrast = visual DRAMA. Thins disappear in poor printing --> demands quality --> signals luxury.
- **Didot (1784):** Hairline thins = fragility = preciousness. ONLY works in premium printing.
- **Futura (1927):** Near-perfect geometry with subtle optical corrections. "You must betray mathematics to serve the eye."

### Typography as Rhythm
- Horizontal rhythm = spacing (the beat)
- Vertical rhythm = leading (the measure)
- "Color" in typography = overall darkness/lightness of text block (NOT hue)

### The Universal Luxury Signal
**Wide letter-spacing.** 16 of 20 luxury brands studied use it. Spacing = air = expense.

## MATERIALS & CRAFT

### Gold Foil
- Hot stamping: 120-180 degrees C, brass die, $200-$2,000+ per die. Highest quality.
- Cold foil: UV adhesive, no die. Can overprint CMYK.
- Digital foil: lowest cost, lowest quality.

### Paper
- Business card luxury: 400-600 GSM (duplexed/triplexed)
- Premium: Mohawk Superfine (100% cotton), Crane's Lettra, Colorplan (92 colors)
- Blind deboss on soft-touch lamination = ultimate tactile luxury

### Making Digital Feel Physical
- Film grain: 3-5% opacity noise
- Paper texture: 2-5% opacity, multiply blend
- Subtle shadows: 20-40px blur, 5-15% opacity
- Matte film curve: raised black point (output level 10-20)

## PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION

### Dark Moody (Luxury Oracle Direction)
- Single source, side-positioned (45-90 degrees). Low-key ratio 4:1 to 8:1. Color temp 3200-4000K.
- f/2.8-5.6, ISO 100-400, spot metering underexposed 1-2 stops.
- Color grade: desaturate 10-20%. Lift shadows slightly. Shift shadows warm. Matte film curve.
