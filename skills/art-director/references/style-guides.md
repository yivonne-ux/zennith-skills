# Style Guides — Design Philosophy & Aesthetic References

## THE MATHEMATICS OF BEAUTY

### Golden Ratio (phi = 1.618)
- Brain processes phi-proportioned forms faster (53% preference, less viewing time needed)
- Typography scale: 16 → 26 → 42 → 68 → 110px
- Line height: font-size x 1.618
- Spacing system: Fibonacci (8, 13, 21, 34, 55, 89px)
- Layout: content vs sidebar = 1.618:1
- Composition focal point: 38.2% from edge (NOT rule of thirds at 33.3%)

### Sacred Geometry
- Vesica Piscis (1:sqrt(3)): mother of all forms. Mastercard, Chanel, CBS Eye
- Islamic quasicrystals: 5 tiles generate infinite non-repeating patterns (Isfahan 1453, 500 years before Penrose)
- Le Corbusier Modulor: Red series (body proportions) + Blue series (extended), all Fibonacci

### Optical Corrections
- Round shapes overshoot flat by 1-3%
- Pointed shapes overshoot by 3-5%
- Horizontal strokes thinner than vertical of "same" weight
- Optical center = 5% above mathematical center
- "We read with our eyes, not with rulers" -- Tobias Frere-Jones

### Color Mathematics
- Albers' Law: colors change based on context. NEVER choose in isolation
- Complementary = 180 degrees, Split-comp = 150+210 degrees, Triadic = 120+240 degrees
- 60-30-10 rule: dominant / secondary / accent
- Convert to grayscale: if hierarchy is clear, value structure is sound
- Warm colors advance, cool colors recede (physiological -- red focuses behind retina)
- Red = heaviest visual weight. Yellow = lightest.

## DARK FEMININE AESTHETIC (when appropriate)

### Warm Darkness vs Cold Darkness
| WARM (use) | COLD (avoid) |
|------------|-------------|
| Candlelight, amber, golden hour | Blue-black, chrome, fluorescent |
| Velvet, leather, warm wood, aged brass | Steel, concrete, industrial |
| 2700K color temperature | Cool-white |
| A candlelit library | A server room |

### The Three Architects of Darkness
- **McQueen:** Darkness as THEATER -- from history, not fantasy
- **Rick Owens:** "Glunge" -- darkness as HONESTY
- **Ann Demeulemeester:** Darkness as POETRY -- starts with feelings, not mood boards

### Mystery Without Cliche
- DO: Shadows, muted palettes, blur, grain, high contrast
- DON'T: Pentagrams, moons-as-decoration, gothic fonts, heavy symbolism
- Making darkness the ATMOSPHERE, never the SUBJECT

## JAPANESE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### Wabi-Sabi
Imperfection, impermanence, incompleteness. Kintsugi: gold-filled cracks -- the repair IS the beauty.

### Ma (between)
Not absence -- PRESENCE of potential. "Emptiness full of possibilities." Ma is NOT minimalism. Minimalism removes to simplify. Ma creates SPACE to invite imagination.

### Kenya Hara's Exformation
The opposite of information. Makes the familiar unfamiliar. Pose questions, never fully answer.

### The Seven Aesthetic Principles of Zen
1. **Fukinsei** -- asymmetry, irregularity
2. **Kanso** -- simplicity, elimination of clutter
3. **Koko** -- austere sublimity
4. **Shizen** -- naturalness without pretension
5. **Yugen** -- profound subtlety, suggestion
6. **Datsuzoku** -- freedom from habit
7. **Seijaku** -- tranquility, stillness

## ART NOUVEAU -- THE DNA OF ORGANIC LUXURY

### Alphonse Mucha's Principles
- **Line = Structure.** Bold outlines for major forms, fine interior lines for texture
- **Color whispers.** Muted pastels: peach, gold, ochre. Color serves mood, not attention
- **Figure-ornament unity.** The subject EMERGES from decorative elements
- **The Halo.** Circular frame = rising sun + sacred mandorla. Isolates, elevates, sanctifies
- **Borders participate.** NOT containers -- active elements echoing the central figure

### The Whiplash Curve
Follows mathematical logic of natural growth. Has INTENTION in every curve. Never random waviness.
