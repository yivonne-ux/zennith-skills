## THE 5-LAYER CREATIVE PROCESS

**Layer 1: FEEL (before anything visual)**
Ask: "What should someone FEEL when they encounter this brand?" Not what it looks like — what EMOTION should it trigger? Write the feeling in ONE sentence. This controls every decision.

**Layer 2: CONCEPT (the one big idea)**
Every great brand has ONE concept that controls everything.
- Bottega Veneta: "When your own initials are enough" → craft IS identity
- Patagonia: "Don't buy this jacket" → anti-consumption AS consumption
- Aesop: "The apothecary for the thinking person" → intellectual beauty

Your concept must be expressible in one sentence. If it takes a paragraph, it's not a concept.

**Layer 3: SYSTEM (not a logo — a world)**
→ Load references/composition-rules.md for the 12 Laws of Transcendent Brands
A logo is 10% of brand identity. The SYSTEM is the other 90%: marks + color + type + photography + texture + motion + sound + spatial + verbal + pattern. Must generate 1,000 unique executions that all feel like the same brand.

**Layer 4: CRAFT (the 1% that makes the 100%)**
→ Load references/style-guides.md for golden ratio, sacred geometry, optical corrections, color mathematics
- Optical corrections > mathematical precision
- Letter-spacing is the single most powerful luxury signal
- Film grain at 2-5% opacity = analog warmth in digital work

**Layer 5: RESTRAINT (what you remove)**
- Remove every element that doesn't serve the concept. Then remove one more.
- The Row has zero visible branding and is worth $300M
- Margiela's stitches were designed to be REMOVED

## HOW TO USE THIS SKILL

### When Starting ANY Brand/Design Work:

1. **Read the brief. Then question it.** Figure out what they NEED, not what they want.
2. **Define the feeling.** One word. Everything flows from this.
3. **Find the concept.** One sentence. Keep thinking until you can.
4. **Study what exists.** Not to copy — to find the white space.
5. **Make the brave choice.** The one that makes you slightly uncomfortable.
6. **Build the system, not just the mark.** 1,000 coherent touchpoints.
7. **Kill everything that doesn't serve the concept.** Then kill one more thing.
8. **Present with conviction.** ONE idea. If you're showing 5 options, you don't have an answer yet.
9. **Test at extremes.** 16px favicon AND 16-foot billboard. B&W photocopy AND gold foil on black.
10. **Ask: will this last 20 years?** If not, keep working.

### Quality Gate (Before Showing Anything):
- [ ] FEELING test — Does it evoke the right emotion?
- [ ] NAPKIN test — Can you draw it from memory?
- [ ] SQUINT test — Recognizable when blurred?
- [ ] FAX test — Works in black and white?
- [ ] FAVICON test — Holds at 16x16?
- [ ] TIME test — Will it date in 5 years?
- [ ] TASTE test — Would you put it in your own home?
- [ ] CONVICTION test — Would you present ONLY this?
