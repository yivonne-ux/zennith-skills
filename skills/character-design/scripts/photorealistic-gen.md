# Photorealistic Character Generation Guide

## THE #1 RULE: PHOTOREALISTIC = PHOTOGRAPHY, NOT ART

When generating AI influencer characters, you are creating PHOTOGRAPHS of a person, not artwork, not illustrations, not digital paintings.

**Think:** editorial fashion photographer + spiritual/wellness shoot
**NOT:** fantasy artist, concept artist, character designer, illustrator

---

## Prompt Structure (PROVEN WORKING)

```
Editorial fashion photography [shot type] of [character name], [age], [ethnicity/skin].
[Hair description]. [Eye description]. [Expression/pose].
Wearing [clothing - keep minimal, natural, organic].
[Jewelry/accessories - gold, jade, minimal].
[Setting - natural, real location, simple].
[Lighting - natural light, golden hour, window light].
Shot on [camera] with [lens], [depth of field], [color grading].
Photorealistic photograph, real human skin with pores and natural imperfections.
NOT illustration, NOT cartoon, NOT CG render, NOT anime, NOT digital painting, NOT fantasy.
```

## MANDATORY Negative Keywords (include in EVERY prompt)

```
NOT illustration, NOT cartoon, NOT CG render, NOT anime, NOT digital painting, NOT fantasy, NOT concept art, NOT character sheet, NOT stylized
```

## MANDATORY Positive Keywords (include in EVERY prompt)

```
Photorealistic photograph, real human skin with pores, editorial photography quality, natural lighting
```

## What Makes It Look Like a PHOTO vs CARTOON

| Photo (CORRECT) | Cartoon (WRONG) |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Natural sunlight, window light | Mystical glow, aura, neon |
| White linen, beige silk, earth tones | Jade robes, gold embroidery, crystal crowns |
| Gold hoop earrings, bangles | Fantasy jewelry, magical artifacts |
| Sitting on floor, couch, real location | Floating, temple, cosmic background |
| Messy bun, natural curls | Elaborate updos, silver-white fantasy hair |
| Real skin texture, pores, freckles | Smooth porcelain, glowing skin |
| 85mm f/1.4, bokeh background | Sharp everything, illustrated background |
| Warm color grading | Oversaturated, fantasy palette |

### Lock Process
1. Round 1: Get the FACE right (3-5 variations)
2. Round 2: Lock face, test different outfits (one change only)
3. Round 3: Lock face + wardrobe, test settings/scenes
4. LOCK: Save style seed, save prompt, save reference images
5. All future gens: use locked images as reference + same prompt structure

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER DO THESE)

1. **Crystal crowns, jade robes, cosmic auras** → These make it look like a video game character
2. **Silver/white hair** → Unless explicitly requested, this screams "fantasy character"
3. **"Sacred futurism aesthetic"** → This phrase produces illustration, not photos
4. **"Character sheet" or "reference sheet"** → Produces flat illustration with callouts
5. **Multiple poses in one image** → Produces illustration grid, not photography
6. **Elaborate backgrounds** → Temples, galaxies, mystical chambers = instant fantasy feel
7. **Over-describing clothing** → "Gold embroidered celestial robes with pre-Hispanic symbols" = costume, not fashion

## Key Principle

**Less is more.** The most photorealistic results come from SIMPLE descriptions of REAL things. A woman in white linen with gold earrings sitting by a window. That's it. The mystical energy comes from the EXPRESSION and LIGHTING, not from fantasy props.
