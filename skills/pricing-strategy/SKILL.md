# Pricing Strategy -- F&B, Wellness, and D2C Brands

Expert in pricing and monetization strategy for consumer brands, with deep focus on F&B meal delivery, wellness subscriptions, supplements, and direct-to-consumer products. Designs pricing that captures value, drives retention, and aligns with customer willingness to pay.

## When to Use

- Setting prices for a new product, menu, or subscription plan
- Restructuring pricing tiers or meal plan options
- Considering a price increase
- Launching a new brand or product line
- Evaluating delivery fee strategy
- Designing bundle or combo pricing
- Comparing against competitors

## Before Starting

1. Load brand DNA: `~/.openclaw/brands/{brand}/DNA.json` -- understand positioning, target audience, and brand tier
2. Gather context below (ask if not provided)

### 1. Business Context
- What type of product? (meal delivery, supplements, packaged food, wellness service, D2C product)
- What is current pricing (if any)?
- What is the target market? (health-conscious professionals, families, fitness enthusiasts, budget-conscious)
- What is the sales channel? (WhatsApp, Shopify, marketplace, in-store, hybrid)

### 2. Value and Competition
- What is the primary value delivered? (convenience, health outcomes, taste, weight management)
- What alternatives do customers consider? (cooking at home, other meal delivery, restaurant takeaway, GrabFood)
- How do local competitors price?

### 3. Current Performance
- What is the current conversion rate?
- What is the average order value and reorder rate?
- Any feedback on pricing from customers?

### 4. Goals
- Optimizing for growth, revenue, or profitability?
- Moving upmarket (premium) or expanding downmarket (accessible)?

## Pricing Fundamentals

### The Three Pricing Axes

**1. Packaging** -- What is included at each tier?
- Meals per week, variety, add-ons, delivery frequency
- How tiers differ from each other

**2. Pricing Metric** -- What do you charge for?
- Per meal, per day, per week, per box, flat subscription
- How price scales with value and volume

**3. Price Point** -- How much do you charge?
- The actual ringgit/dollar amounts
- Perceived value vs. cost to deliver

### Value-Based Pricing

Price should be based on value delivered, not cost to serve:

- **Customer's perceived value** -- The ceiling (what they would pay for the outcome)
- **Your price** -- Between alternatives and perceived value
- **Next best alternative** -- The floor (home cooking cost, competitor price, GrabFood equivalent)
- **Your cost to serve** -- Only a baseline, not the basis for pricing

**Key insight:** Price between the next best alternative and perceived value. For wellness/health brands, the "value" includes health outcomes, not just the food.

## Value Metrics for F&B and Wellness

### What is a Value Metric?

The unit you charge for -- it should scale with the value customers receive.

### Common Value Metrics

| Metric | Best For | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Per meal | Meal delivery, bento | MIRRA RM15/meal |
| Per day | Full-day meal plans | 3 meals + snacks/day |
| Per week/box | Subscription boxes | Weekly wellness box |
| Per serving | Supplements, drinks | Per sachet, per bottle |
| Per session | Consultations, coaching | Nutritionist session |
| Per kg/unit | Packaged products | Granola per 500g |
| Flat monthly | All-inclusive plans | Unlimited meal plan |

### Choosing Your Value Metric

Ask: "As a customer uses more of [metric], do they get more value?"
- Per meal for bento/delivery -- yes, each meal saves cooking time and delivers nutrition
- Per month for supplements -- yes, consistent use drives health results
- Flat fee for consultations -- careful, may not scale with value

## Tier Structure for Consumer Brands

### Good-Better-Best Framework

**Good tier (Entry):** Basic offering, lower commitment, accessible price
- Example: 3 meals/week, standard menu, self-pickup option

**Better tier (Recommended):** Full experience, best value per unit
- Example: 5 meals/week, full menu access, delivery included, 1 consultation/month

**Best tier (Premium):** Everything, highest convenience and personalization
- Example: 7 meals/week, custom macros, priority delivery, weekly nutritionist check-in

### Tier Differentiation Levers

- **Volume**: Fewer vs. more meals/products per period
- **Variety**: Limited vs. full menu access
- **Customization**: Standard vs. personalized (macros, allergies, preferences)
- **Delivery**: Self-pickup vs. scheduled delivery vs. priority/express
- **Support**: Self-serve vs. WhatsApp support vs. dedicated nutritionist
- **Add-ons**: Snacks, drinks, supplements, consultation sessions

### Subscription vs. One-Off

| Model | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|
| Weekly subscription | Predictable revenue, production planning | Commitment anxiety |
| Monthly subscription | Higher LTV, lower churn | Larger upfront cost |
| One-off ordering | Low friction, try-before-commit | Unpredictable demand |
| Hybrid (subscribe + a la carte) | Best of both | Operational complexity |

**Recommendation for most GAIA brands:** Hybrid model. One-off to acquire, subscription to retain. Offer 10-15% discount for subscription commitment.

## Pricing Research Methods

### Informal (Low Cost, Quick)

- **WhatsApp poll**: "Would you pay RMX for Y?" -- fast signal from existing audience
- **A/B test on Shopify**: Show different prices to different traffic segments
- **Menu price testing**: Introduce a new item at a test price, measure uptake
- **Competitor mystery shopping**: Order from 5 competitors, compare value-for-money

### Structured Research

**Van Westendorp Method (4 questions):**
1. At what price would this be too expensive? (would not consider)
2. At what price would this be too cheap? (question quality)
3. At what price is it getting expensive but you might still consider?
4. At what price is it a bargain?

Analyze intersections to find the optimal pricing zone.

**MaxDiff Analysis:**
- Show sets of features/benefits
- Ask: Most important? Least important?
- Results inform which features justify premium pricing

## When to Raise Prices

### Signs It Is Time

**Market signals:**
- Competitors have raised prices
- Customers do not flinch at price
- "So cheap for what you get!" feedback
- Raw material costs have increased

**Business signals:**
- Very high conversion/reorder rates
- Very low churn (subscribers staying 3+ months)
- Waitlist or capacity constraints
- Margins shrinking due to cost increases

**Product signals:**
- Significant value added (new menu items, better packaging, faster delivery)
- Brand reputation and trust have grown
- Certifications obtained (organic, halal, etc.)

### Price Increase Strategies

1. **Grandfather existing** -- New price for new customers only (protect loyals)
2. **Advance notice** -- Announce 2-4 weeks out via WhatsApp/email
3. **Tied to value** -- Raise price but add visible value (new packaging, extra item, faster delivery)
4. **Plan restructure** -- Change plans entirely so there is no direct comparison
5. **Shrinkflation alternative** -- Rather than reducing portion, increase price transparently (builds trust)

## Pricing Psychology for Consumer Brands

- **Anchoring**: Show the premium option first to make the standard feel affordable
- **Decoy effect**: Middle tier should be the obvious best value
- **Per-meal breakdown**: "Just RM12/meal" is more palatable than "RM360/month"
- **Round pricing**: RM50 vs. RM49 -- round for premium positioning, charm for value
- **Bundle savings**: Show the per-unit discount when buying bundles
- **Free delivery threshold**: "Free delivery on orders above RM80" drives AOV
- **Loss framing**: "Save RM120/month vs. eating out" -- compare to the alternative

## Delivery Fee Strategy

Delivery fees are a major conversion lever for F&B brands:

| Strategy | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|
| Free delivery (built into price) | Premium brands, subscription plans |
| Free above threshold | Drive higher AOV, works well for D2C |
| Flat fee | Simple, predictable for customer |
| Distance-based | Fair but complex, may deter far customers |
| Free for subscribers | Incentivize subscription commitment |

## Pricing Checklist

### Before Setting Prices
- [ ] Loaded brand DNA and understood positioning
- [ ] Researched competitor pricing (at least 5 competitors)
- [ ] Identified primary value metric
- [ ] Calculated cost to serve (COGS + delivery + packaging)
- [ ] Defined target customer personas and their willingness to pay
- [ ] Mapped features/benefits to tiers

### Pricing Structure
- [ ] Chosen number of tiers (2-3 recommended for most brands)
- [ ] Differentiated tiers clearly with meaningful differences
- [ ] Set price points based on research, not gut feel
- [ ] Created subscription discount strategy (10-15% off one-off)
- [ ] Planned delivery fee approach
- [ ] Built in margin for promotions without going below cost
