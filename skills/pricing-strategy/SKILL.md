Expert in pricing and monetization strategy for consumer brands, with deep focus on F&B meal delivery, wellness subscriptions, supplements, and direct-to-consumer products.

## Pricing Fundamentals

### The Three Pricing Axes
- **Packaging** -- What is included at each tier
- **Pricing Metric** -- What you charge for (per meal, per week, flat subscription)
- **Price Point** -- The actual ringgit/dollar amounts

### Value-Based Pricing
Price between the next best alternative (floor) and customer's perceived value (ceiling). For wellness/health brands, "value" includes health outcomes, not just the food.

## Value Metrics for F&B and Wellness

| Metric | Best For | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Per meal | Meal delivery, bento | MIRRA RM15/meal |
| Per day | Full-day meal plans | 3 meals + snacks/day |
| Per week/box | Subscription boxes | Weekly wellness box |
| Per serving | Supplements, drinks | Per sachet, per bottle |
| Per session | Consultations, coaching | Nutritionist session |
| Per kg/unit | Packaged products | Granola per 500g |
| Flat monthly | All-inclusive plans | Unlimited meal plan |

**Choosing test:** "As a customer uses more of [metric], do they get more value?" If yes, it's a good metric.

## Tier Structure

### Good-Better-Best Framework

| Tier | What | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| **Good** (Entry) | Basic, lower commitment, accessible | 3 meals/week, standard menu, self-pickup |
| **Better** (Recommended) | Full experience, best value/unit | 5 meals/week, full menu, delivery, 1 consult/month |
| **Best** (Premium) | Everything, highest convenience | 7 meals/week, custom macros, priority delivery, weekly nutritionist |

### Differentiation Levers
Volume, variety, customization, delivery, support, add-ons

### Subscription vs. One-Off

| Model | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|
| Weekly subscription | Predictable revenue, production planning | Commitment anxiety |
| Monthly subscription | Higher LTV, lower churn | Larger upfront cost |
| One-off ordering | Low friction, try-before-commit | Unpredictable demand |
| Hybrid | Best of both | Operational complexity |

**Recommendation:** Hybrid model. One-off to acquire, subscription to retain. 10-15% discount for subscription.

## Delivery Fee Strategy

| Strategy | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|
| Free delivery (built into price) | Premium brands, subscription plans |
| Free above threshold | Drive higher AOV, works well for D2C |
| Flat fee | Simple, predictable for customer |
| Distance-based | Fair but complex, may deter far customers |
| Free for subscribers | Incentivize subscription commitment |
