## 3. KEY QUALITY DIFFERENTIATORS

### Grass-Fed vs Grain-Fed
- **USDA data:** New Zealand liver has 50-450% MORE vitamins than US conventional liver
- In the US, "grass-fed" cattle can be finished on alfalfa pellets in feedlots (loophole)
- **Best sourcing:** New Zealand or Australia (year-round pasture, no feedlots, strict regulations)
- Argentina is also good but less recognized in market

### Freeze-Dried vs Desiccated (Heat-Dried)
- **Freeze-drying** preserves bioactive compounds, enzymes, and heat-sensitive nutrients
- **Heat desiccation/spray drying** damages fragile compounds and nutrients
- **Best practice:** 48-hour slow freeze-drying + cryo-milling (cold milling prevents heat/friction damage)
- "Non-defatted" is important -- some brands remove fat, losing fat-soluble vitamins

### Third-Party Testing Hierarchy
1. **Informed Sport** (gold standard for athletes) -- Heart & Soil has this
2. **NSF Certified for Sport**
3. **USP Verified**
4. **Independent lab COAs** (Certificate of Analysis)
5. No testing (red flag)

## 3. FDA REGULATORY LANDSCAPE

### Classification
- Anti-snoring mouthpieces = **Class II medical devices** (FDA product code: LRK)
- Require **510(k) clearance** (demonstrate "substantial equivalence" to a predicate device)
- Must register with FDA and follow federal regulations to legally sell in the US

### Can You Sell Without FDA Clearance?
- **Technically illegal** in the United States
- **Reality:** Many brands (Trevalisse, ZComfort, dozens of Amazon sellers) sell without clearance
- **Risk level:** Low enforcement for small DTC brands, BUT:
  - FDA can issue warning letters
  - Amazon may pull listings
  - Competitors can report you
  - Liability in case of injury claims

### 510(k) Clearance Pathway
- Cost: $12,000-$15,000 (FDA fee) + $20,000-$80,000 (testing/documentation)
- Timeline: 3-12 months
- Requires: Predicate device comparison, biocompatibility testing, performance testing
- **Strategy:** Launch without FDA clearance initially (like Trevalisse does), market as "sleep aid" not "medical device," then pursue 510(k) once revenue justifies cost

### For Non-US Markets (Malaysia/SEA)
- Malaysia: Medical Device Authority (MDA) registration
- Requirements vary by country but generally less stringent than FDA
- CE marking for European market

## 4. MATERIALS COMPARISON

| Material | Pros | Cons | Used By |
|----------|------|------|---------|
| **Medical-grade silicone** | Softest, most comfortable, hypoallergenic, tasteless | More expensive, less structural rigidity | ZQuiet, tongue retainers |
| **EVA (Ethylene-Vinyl Acetate)** | Good moldability, comfortable, affordable, FDA-approved for dental | Less durable than thermoplastic, can wear faster | SleepTight, many Chinese OEM |
| **Thermoplastic (copolymer)** | Most durable, excellent fit via boil-and-bite, good structural support | Harder feel, can cause more jaw discomfort initially | SnoreRx, VitalSleep |
| **Dual-laminate** | Soft inner + hard outer = comfort + structure | More complex to manufacture | SleepTight |

**Recommendation:** Use **medical-grade thermoplastic** with a **soft silicone inner liner** -- combines structural support with comfort. This dual-material approach is a premium differentiator.
