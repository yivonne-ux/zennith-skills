## 3. Top Performing Content Patterns

**Hook formulas (slide 1 / caption line 1):**
- `"Signs you have [X] wound:"` → list format, massively saved
- `"This is what [trauma response] actually looks like:"` → pattern recognition
- `"If you grew up with [X], you learned to:"` → identity resonance
- `"Most people don't realize [counterintuitive truth]"` → disrupts assumption
- `"You're not [negative label]. You're [reframe]."` → validation hook

**What drives saves:**
- Lists of 7–10 actionable "signs" or "symptoms" — people save to revisit
- Reframes ("this isn't weakness, this is a trauma response")
- Permission-giving statements that reduce shame
- Frameworks they can apply to themselves instantly

**CTAs that work for them:**
- "Save this for when you need a reminder"
- "Share this with someone who needs to hear it"
- "Which of these resonates? Tell me in the comments"
- "Tag someone healing alongside you"

## 4. Engagement Tactics

**Comment drivers:**
- Open-ended identity questions: "Which one hits hardest?"
- Vulnerability prompts: "What did you learn about yourself today?"
- Recognition triggers: posts that make people say "this is literally me"

**Stories/Reels integration:**
- Stories used for polls ("are you a people pleaser?"), Q&A boxes, behind-the-scenes
- Reels are primarily repurposed from talking-head clips on YouTube/podcast — short, direct, 60–90 sec
- Reels used for reach; carousels used for depth/saves

**Community building:**
- Strong "SelfHealers" community identity — followers self-identify as part of a movement
- Regular use of "we" language ("we weren't taught this")
- Collaboration with similar educators (but infrequent)
