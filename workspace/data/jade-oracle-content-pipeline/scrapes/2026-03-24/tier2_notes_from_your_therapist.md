## 2. Recent Content Themes & Formats

### Topics (recurring, evergreen)
- **Childhood emotional neglect (CEN)** — her signature topic; posts on how CEN shows up in adult relationships drive the most saves
- **Parentification** — children taking on parents' emotional labor
- **Grief and loss** — not just death, also relationship grief, identity grief
- **Boundaries** — practical framing ("what growth actually looks like")
- **Adult friendships & loneliness** — difficulty with closeness after relational trauma
- **Self-worth and self-abandonment**
- **Inner child work**

### Dominant Format
- **Single image** of a handwritten note — this is 90%+ of her content
- Occasionally a photograph of a physical note taped to a surface
- Minimal use of Reels or carousels
- Caption is short (1–3 sentences), non-explanatory — the note IS the content

### Visual Style
- **Analog, lo-fi aesthetic** — ruled notebook paper, Post-it notes, sometimes torn pages
- No brand colors, no Canva polish, no design system
- Deliberately imperfect handwriting = psychological authenticity signal
- Warm, slightly overexposed photography (natural light on paper)

## 3. Top Performing Content Patterns

### Hooks that drive saves
Her notes function as the hook — first words are always emotionally naming something:
- *"It makes sense that you struggle to..."*
- *"One sign you experienced emotional neglect as a child..."*
- *"This is what growth actually looks like:"* (followed by a list)
- *"You are allowed to..."*
- Naming a specific behavior pattern and validating it

**Save trigger formula:** Validation of an experience the reader couldn't name before + normalizing language + therapeutic framing that doesn't pathologize.

### What drives saves/shares in this niche
1. **Identity recognition** — "This is me" content that people screenshot and send to a partner or friend
2. **Naming invisible wounds** — CEN, parentification, emotional labor — things without common vocabulary
3. **Permission-giving language** — "You are allowed to…", "It's okay that..."
4. **List-format notes** — scannable, shareable, quotable
5. **Specificity over generality** — "struggle to receive care from a partner" beats "struggle with relationships"

### CTAs
Almost none. She rarely uses explicit CTAs. The content is so save-worthy it doesn't need them. Occasionally: "save this if it resonates." No link-in-bio funnels beyond book sales.

## 4. Engagement Tactics

## 5. Viral Post Examples (High Save/Share Posts)

**Post 1 — Childhood Emotional Neglect in Adult Relationships**
> Handwritten note describing how adults who grew up emotionally neglected struggle to let others get close, even when they desperately want connection.
*Why it works:* Names a precise emotional experience, validates the contradiction, gives language to something previously wordless. Gets shared between partners.

**Post 2 — What Growth Actually Looks Like**
> A list-format note with ~8 items: "respecting others' boundaries," "letting people express themselves without interrupting," etc.
*Why it works:* List format = saveable reference. Reframes growth as relational behavior, not achievement.

**Post 3 — Parentification**
> Note about children who learn to manage a parent's emotional needs, and how it affects their adult relationships.
*Why it works:* Names a specific dynamic with a clinical term, but explains it accessibly. People share with siblings ("this was us").

**Post 4 — Permission to Grieve Non-Death Losses**
> Note validating grief for relationships, versions of yourself, opportunities that didn't happen.
*Why it works:* Opens a category most people didn't know they had permission to mourn. Very high send-to-friend rate.

**Post 5 — The "You Are Allowed To" Note**
> A short note granting permission for a specific behavior (leaving, changing your mind, needing more, etc.)
*Why it works:* Punchy, screenshot-ready, feels like a direct message from a trusted authority figure.

## Strategic Takeaway for Jade Oracle

NFYT owns **validation of invisible wounds through analog intimacy**. She has a loyal audience but is a **content creator stuck in 2018 Instagram** — static images, no Reels, no community mechanics, no spiritual layer.

Jade Oracle's attack vector: **"NFYT tells you what happened. Jade tells you why it's happening, what cycle you're in, and what to do next."**

The emotional entry point is identical (validation, naming pain, permission-giving language). The differentiation is **agency + spirituality + ritual** — moving from wound to wisdom to action.
