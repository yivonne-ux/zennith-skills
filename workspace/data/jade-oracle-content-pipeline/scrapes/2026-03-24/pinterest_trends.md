## 02 — ORACLE DECK DESIGN

**Current Visual Trends**
Two dominant poles pulling in the same direction: (1) **Contemporary Folk** — traditional symbols reimagined with textile patterns, rhythmic compositions, pattern-forward; (2) **Minimal Abstract** — sparse symbols, heavy negative space, cave-art simplicity. Dark ground decks (deep forest green, plum, navy) are gaining over light backgrounds. Metallic gold/copper lettering on dark grounds is a strong visual signal of quality.

**Top Pin Formats**
- Single card close-up with 1-line meaning (high save, educational value)
- Full deck spread as mood board (aspirational, high re-pin)
- Process/behind-the-scenes (sketch → final card) — strong on Idea Pins

**Aesthetic Direction**
Oracle design is moving toward symbolic clarity paired with emotional depth. The card needs to communicate meaning intuitively — not hide it. Folk art rhythm meets meditative stillness. Accessible to beginners, resonant for practitioners.

**Adaptable Ideas for Jade Oracle**

2. Dark-ground card mockup: jade symbols on plum/near-black background with gold accent type — position Jade Oracle as its own deck universe
3. Symbolic alphabet series — one symbol per post (moon, serpent, stone, etc.) explained through the Jade Oracle lens

**Color/Mood Palette**
Plum #351E28, Jade #3D7A5E, warm gold, forest green, off-black

Ornate borders, crest-style compositions, heavy serif lettering, but executed with modern restraint. Bold B&W line art remains strong. Collage tarot (photography fragments + illustration + symbol overlays) is accelerating as a more accessible creator format.

**Top Pin Formats**

There's a shift from mass witchy-pastel to more personal, editorial, almost archive-quality imagery. The feeling is studied, quiet, and confident rather than performative.

## 04 — MINIMALIST SPIRITUAL QUOTES

**Current Visual Trends**
Pure cold minimalism is being actively rejected in 2026. "Welcoming minimalism" or "neo-minimalism" is its replacement — same clean composition, but: warm natural texture backgrounds (linen, paper grain, stone), serif fonts (never sans-serif for spiritual content), subtle film grain or ink texture overlays. One decorative element alongside type: a thin rule, a pressed botanical, a moon phase glyph.

**Top Pin Formats**
Textured quote cards on warm backgrounds (cream, muted sage, stone gray) outperform plain white cards significantly. Short quotes — under 12 words — in large serif type on a simple textured background are the most-saved format in this category.

**Aesthetic Direction**
The look is "found in a journal, not generated by an app." Organic, analog, human. Subtle imperfections signal authenticity — an ink texture, an imperfect hand-drawn rule, a botanical impression. Polished-but-predictable is being replaced by intentional-but-imperfect.

**Adaptable Ideas for Jade Oracle**
1. Linen-texture oracle messages — Jade Oracle's daily wisdom in large serif type on warm cream/linen texture; add jade-green thin rule below
2. Handwritten accent series — oracle insight in printed serif, with a handwritten Korean or calligraphic word accent
3. Paper-torn or wax seal editorial: message cards that feel like found objects — oracle wisdom on aged paper with wax seal visual motif

**Color/Mood Palette**
Warm cream, linen, muted sage, deep jade, burgundy text
`keywords: analog · handcrafted · unhurried · journaled · warm quiet · legible depth`

## 05 — KOREAN WELLNESS AESTHETIC

**Current Visual Trends**
"Bloom Skin" is replacing "glass skin" as the defining K-beauty visual — soft luminous matte skin tones rather than reflective gloss. This translates visually to softer, more analog photography. Hanok-inspired architecture is driving the spatial aesthetic: warm wood, curved rooflines, paper screen light diffusion, aged stone courtyards. Korean ceramics (celadon, earthenware) used as styling props signal authenticity. The framework is "wholeness and daily ritual as identity" — not occasional indulgence.

**Top Pin Formats**
Portrait photography against hanok architecture or plant-framed café windows. Flat-lay with Korean ceramics + botanicals + wellness objects. Infographic-style skin/body ritual breakdowns.

**Aesthetic Direction**
Slow, deliberate, and complete. The 2026 Korean wellness visual language communicates that self-care is unhurried and identity-deep — not a routine but a worldview. Younger audiences (15–24) are driving "early wellness" — proactive, ritual-first, deeply visual. The mood is: calm, luminous, and rooted.

**Adaptable Ideas for Jade Oracle**
1. Morning ritual series — oracle card draw photographed with Korean tea ware, celadon bowl, botanicals on warm wood; "begin intentionally" messaging
2. Bloom-skin portrait — editorial portrait of a woman in muted earthy/jade tones against hanok door or paper screen; oracle insight as caption
3. "Small altar" flat lay — Korean ceramics, jade stone, dried flower, single oracle card arranged as a personal ritual space
