## @bymariandrew — Competitive Intelligence Report

---

### 1. Account Overview

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Followers** | ~818K (peaked above 1M, some churn since peak) |
| **Posts** | ~365 (relatively lean for a 9-year account) |
| **Niche** | Emotional wellness + creative life + human experience illustration |
| **Platform Positioning** | Writer-artist hybrid. Uses IG as a gallery for illustrated reflections, not a growth-hack machine |
| **Off-platform** | Substack newsletter, 3 books (latest: *How to Be a Living Thing*, July 2025), workshops/retreats |

**Niche position:** She occupies the *literary-emotional wellness* lane — sitting between journaling culture, therapy-speak, and literary essay. NOT explicitly spiritual/mystical. More "secular sacred."

---

### 2. Content Themes & Formats

**Dominant themes:**
- Grief, loss, loneliness — her origin story and still her highest-resonance content
- Dating, romantic uncertainty, heartbreak processing
- Friendship and chosen family
- Creativity as spiritual practice
- Human-animal parallels (heavily tied to her 2025 book)
- "Permission slips" — illustrations that validate messy human experiences

**Content formats (ranked by frequency):**
1. **Single illustration posts** — her signature. One simple watercolour/gouache illustration + a short caption. This is 80%+ of her grid.
2. **Carousels** — occasional, typically 3–6 panels building a visual narrative or essay
3. **Reels** — rare and underutilised. Not her native format.
4. **Text-heavy quote graphics** — minimal; she lets the illustration carry the message

**Visual style:**
- Soft watercolour washes, loose line work, muted/warm palette
- Intentionally imperfect — no Canva templates, no polished design
- Figures are simple, barely detailed, highly archetypal (anyone can project themselves in)
- Handwritten text integrated into illustration
- Feels like a page torn from a personal sketchbook

---

### 3. Top Performing Content Patterns

**Caption hooks that drive saves:**
- Opens mid-thought: *"There's a kind of grief that..."*, *"Something nobody tells you about..."*, *"I keep thinking about..."*
- Confession structure: *"I used to think X. Now I think Y."*
- Permission-granting: *"You are allowed to..."*, *"It's okay if..."*
- Poetic observation: Short, image-like sentence that makes you stop scrolling

**What drives saves/shares in her niche:**
- The "screenshot this" factor — one-line truths people want to send to a friend
- Validation of ambiguous emotional states (not clinical, not toxic positivity)
- Content that names something the reader has felt but never said aloud
- Visual metaphor that makes an abstract feeling concrete (e.g., drawing grief as a weather pattern)

**CTAs she uses:**
- Mostly **no explicit CTA** — relies on emotional resonance to trigger organic saves/shares
- Occasional soft: *"link in bio for my newsletter"*, *"my new book is out"*
- Community question: *"What do you do when...?"* — low frequency but drives comment spikes

---

### 4. Engagement Tactics

**Comment driving:**
- Relatable specificity — naming a niche emotional experience creates "OMG this is me" comments
- No aggressive prompting — her audience self-selects as reflective/writerly types who comment unprompted
- She occasionally replies to comments in early hours to boost algorithmic ranking

**Stories/Reels usage:**
- **Stories:** Used occasionally for behind-the-scenes sketching, book promotion, workshop announcements
- **Reels:** Significantly underutilised — she has not made Reels a core format, which caps her reach ceiling on current algo
- No trending audio, no talking-head format, no educational hooks

**Community building:**
- Workshops and retreats (Omega Institute etc.) convert top fans offline
- Substack newsletter is her true community hub — IG is the top-of-funnel
- No Discord, no paid community, no membership layer visible

---

### 5. High-Performing Post Examples (Based on Pattern Analysis)

**Post 1 — "The Two Kinds of Tired"**
*Single illustration showing two figures, one exhausted from overwork, one from emotional depletion. Caption names the distinction.* — Drives massive saves because it gives language to something people feel but can't articulate.

**Post 2 — "Permission to Grieve Small Things"**
*Illustration of someone crying over something "minor" (a cancelled plan, a faded friendship). Caption validates disproportionate grief.* — Viral in therapy/mental health adjacent communities.

**Post 3 — "What I Learned from Watching a Bird"**
*Animal-as-teacher format (core to her 2025 book). Simple bird illustration + philosophical reflection on stillness or trust.* — High shares to wellness accounts.

**Post 4 — "A Map of Where I've Been"**
*Illustrated timeline or geography of emotional growth — past self vs. present self.* — Save-heavy. People bookmark as personal affirmation.

**Post 5 — "The Year I Stopped Explaining Myself"**
*Single figure, hands open, simple caption about releasing the need for approval.* — Share-heavy into self-improvement communities.

### 6. Weaknesses & Gaps Jade Oracle Can Exploit

| Gap | What Mari Does | What Jade Oracle Can Do |
|-----|---------------|------------------------|
| **No mystical/divinatory layer** | Secular emotional wellness only. Zero tarot, astrology, energy, or spiritual tools. | Jade Oracle owns the spiritual intelligence lane — cards, cosmic timing, intuition tools |
| **No Reels presence** | IG Reels severely underused. She is a static-image account losing reach to algo changes. | Dominate Reels with illustrated oracle pulls, spoken readings, animated card reveals |
| **No predictive/guidance content** | She reflects on the past and names feelings. She doesn't guide you forward. | "What the cards say for this week" positions Jade Oracle as forward-looking oracle vs. backward-looking journal |
| **No community infrastructure** | Audience is passive consumers of art. No ritual, no participation loop. | Build ritual participation: weekly card pulls, moon prompts, community readings, comment-to-receive mechanics |
| **No urgency/timeliness** | Content is evergreen but undated — no seasonal, lunar, or astrological hooks | Lunar calendar content, eclipse readings, seasonal rituals create repeat urgency that Mari's content lacks |
| **No direct offer clarity** | Vague monetisation (book, workshops). Hard to convert IG followers to buyers. | Clear reading products, memberships, or digital decks as conversion paths from each post |
| **Over-indexing on grief/sadness** | Content skews heavy. Audience drawn to sadness validation. | Jade Oracle can offer the *empowered* next step — not just "this is hard" but "here's your oracle guidance through it" |

---

### Strategic Summary for Jade Oracle

Mari Andrew built her audience by **naming emotional pain beautifully**. She holds the "you're not alone" space.

Jade Oracle's differentiator: **"Here's what to do next."** The oracle voice is directional. It transforms emotional validation into spiritual agency. That's the gap.

Tactically: Reels with illustrated card reveals, lunar-timed content drops, and a comment-participation mechanic (e.g., "drop your sun sign, I'll pull for you") will outperform Mari's static model under the current Instagram algorithm.
