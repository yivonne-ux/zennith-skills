# Comprehensive Design Resource Catalog
**Last updated: March 2026**

A categorized guide to the best resources used by world-class designers for inspiration, assets, typography, color, production, education, and motion.

---

## 1. DESIGN INSPIRATION PLATFORMS

### Portfolio and Showcase Sites

**[Behance](https://www.behance.net/)** — Adobe's complete portfolio platform. Best for long-form project documentation, case studies, and process breakdowns. Projects rank well in Google search, making it strong for professional credibility. Strategic use: reverse-engineer how successful designers structure their narrative flow — problem, role, process, impact.

**[Dribbble](https://dribbble.com/)** — Originally a platform for sharing "shots" (small visual previews), now a thriving community for inspiration, networking, and jobs. Best for quick visual feedback and trend-spotting. Power user tip: create "buckets" (collections of shots from other designers) organized by category. Dual-platform strategy: tease visuals on Dribbble, link back to full Behance case studies.

**[Awwwards](https://www.awwwards.com/)** — The gold standard for web design excellence. Recognizes and promotes talent from the best developers, designers, and web agencies in the world. Use it to study cutting-edge web interactions, animation, and layout innovation.

### Editorial and Industry Coverage

**[It's Nice That](https://www.itsnicethat.com/)** — Reaches over a million people monthly. Updated daily covering graphic design, illustration, photography, art. Features bi-annual magazine (Printed Pages), summer symposium (Here), monthly Nicer Tuesdays talks. Their annual "Review of the Year" (Top 25 Graphic Design) is a must-read.

**[Brand New (UnderConsideration)](https://www.underconsideration.com/brandnew/)** — The definitive resource for brand identity reviews. Updated daily. Run by Bryony Gomez-Palacio and Armin Vit. Content types: "Reviewed" (in-depth opinion), "Spotted" (acknowledgment), "Linked" (quirky/news), "Revisited" (checking back 1yr+ later). Essential for branding work.

**[The Dieline](https://thedieline.com/)** — The world's leading packaging design resource. Covers packaging design trends, typography in packaging, material innovation, structural design.

### Visual Inspiration and Curation

**[Muzli](https://muz.li/)** — Browser extension (new-tab replacement) that delivers design stories and inspiration. Mines 150+ sources including Dribbble, Behance, Designspiration, TechCrunch. Best for passive discovery — every new tab becomes an inspiration feed.

**[Designspiration](https://www.designspiration.com/)** — Image and color search engine for creating mood boards. Search by color (select up to 5 colors to find matching designs) — uniquely powerful for palette-driven exploration.

**[Pinterest](https://www.pinterest.com/)** — Designers use it as a rapid moodboard tool. Algorithm excels at surfacing visually similar content. Best practice: private boards per project, pin broadly first, then curate down. "More like this" feature is the real power.

**[Are.na](https://www.are.na/)** — "Playlists, but for ideas." Intentionally lacks likes, shares, and comments to encourage genuine curation. Users create Channels containing Blocks (images, links, PDFs, videos, text). Blocks can exist in multiple Channels. Best for long-term knowledge building.

**[Savee](https://savee.com/)** — Elegant creative moodboard app built around a clean, infinite grid. No ads, no clutter, 1M+ users. AI-powered recommendations, search by image, trending collections. Best for the initial, exploratory moodboarding phase.

### Niche Galleries

- **[Godly](https://godly.website/)** — Curated collection of beautiful websites
- **[Mobbin](https://mobbin.com/)** — Mobile and web design patterns (freemium)
- **[Siteinspire](https://www.siteinspire.com/)** — Curated web design gallery by style, type, subject

## 2. TYPOGRAPHY RESOURCES

### Free Font Libraries

**[Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/)** — Largest free, open-source font library. All free for commercial use.
- **Sans-serif**: Inter, Roboto, Lato, Montserrat, Open Sans, Poppins, DM Sans
- **Serif**: Merriweather, PT Serif, Lora, Playfair Display
- **Display**: Bebas Neue, Space Grotesk
- Tip: Typewolf's curated list of 40 best Google Fonts is better than browsing the full library.

**[Fontshare](https://fontshare.com/)** (by Indian Type Foundry) — Professional-grade fonts, 100% free for personal and commercial use. Every font is vetted. Smaller library than Google Fonts but higher average quality.

**[Adobe Fonts](https://fonts.adobe.com/)** — Included with Adobe Creative Cloud. Enormous professional library from top foundries. "Similar Fonts" and "Visual Search" features help find alternatives.

### Premium Independent Foundries

**[Klim Type Foundry](https://klim.co.nz/)** — New Zealand, founded 2005 by Kris Sowersby. Notable: Domaine (sharp serif), Calibre (geometric sans), Tiempos (used by National Geographic), Geograph. Premium pricing, unmatched quality.

**[Grilli Type](https://www.grillitype.com/)** — Swiss, est. 2009. Notable: GT Walsheim (ubiquitous in tech), GT Haptik, GT Canon (224 styles across six subfamilies). Also does custom commissions.

**[Commercial Type](https://commercialtype.com/)** — Paul Barnes + Christian Schwartz (NY/London). Used by The Guardian, Vanity Fair. Notable: Graphik (clean sans workhorse), Austin (display serif), Dala Floda. Rooted in editorial design.

**[Pangram Pangram](https://pangrampangram.com/)** — Montreal, 2016. All fonts free to try (desktop trial for mockups). Licenses from $40. Notable: Neue Montreal, Neue Machina, Formula Condensed, Editorial New. Trend-forward, popular with younger designers.

**[Dinamo](https://abcdinamo.com/)** — Berlin. Most intellectually driven and experimental type studio. Releases fonts "like albums." Pioneered value-based licensing (2020). Font Customizer tool lets users customize free. Notable: ABC Diatype, ABC Favorit, ABC Whyte.

**[Future Fonts](https://www.futurefonts.xyz/)** — Marketplace for typefaces in development. Buy at discount, receive all future updates. Great for unique faces before they become ubiquitous.

### Typography Tools and References

**[Typewolf](https://www.typewolf.com/)** — THE best resource for typography recommendations and trend tracking. "Site of the Day" showcases fonts in professional context.

**[Fonts In Use](https://fontsinuse.com/)** — Independent archive of typography indexed by typeface, format, industry, topic. See how fonts perform in real-world applications.

**[FontPair](https://www.fontpair.co/)** — 1,000+ curated font pairing combinations using Google Fonts.

**[Type Scale](https://typescale.net/)** — Generates harmonious typographic scales using mathematical ratios (Major Third, Perfect Fourth, Golden Ratio). Exports CSS/SCSS/JSON.

**[Fontjoy](https://fontjoy.com/)** — AI-powered font pairing generator with contrast slider.

### Best Free Fonts for Social Media Design
- **Headlines**: Bebas Neue, Montserrat Bold/Black, Space Grotesk, DM Serif Display
- **Body text**: Inter, DM Sans, Lato, Open Sans
- **Display/character**: Anybody (playful), SuperDuper (condensed), Malinton (versatile)
- **From Fontshare**: Satoshi, General Sans, Cabinet Grotesk, Clash Display
- **From Pangram Pangram**: Neue Montreal, Editorial New, Right Gothic

## 3. PHOTO AND TEXTURE RESOURCES

### Free Photography
**[Unsplash](https://unsplash.com/)** — Most popular free stock. Clean, modern, natural-light. Check download counts to avoid overused shots.
**[Pexels](https://www.pexels.com/)** — Free high-res photos and videos. More candid/everyday aesthetic. Good complement to Unsplash.

### Premium Photography
**[Stocksy](https://www.stocksy.com/)** — Curated by professional photographers. Focus on natural light, real emotion, thoughtful composition. Co-op model pays artists fairly. Best for brand campaigns needing authenticity.
**[Shutterstock](https://www.shutterstock.com/)** / **[Getty](https://www.gettyimages.com/)** — Largest commercial libraries. Shutterstock = volume pricing; Getty = premium/editorial.

### Texture Resources
**[Subtle Patterns](https://www.toptal.com/designers/subtlepatterns/)** — 500+ free seamless pattern backgrounds. CC licensed. The go-to for subtle backgrounds.
**[Textures.com](https://www.textures.com/)** — Massive high-res photographic textures: wood, metal, fabric, stone, paper. Free tier with daily limits.
**[Transparent Textures](https://www.transparenttextures.com/)** — Preview textures with custom background colors in browser. Instant CSS code.

### Grain/Noise
- [TinkPro Noise Generator](https://tinkpro.com/noise-generator/) — Seamless Perlin/Simplex noise textures
- Monochromatic Gaussian noise at 2-5% as final post-processing step
- Paper texture overlays from TilingTextures, MyFreeTextures

### Organic Textures
- [Lisa Glanz](https://www.lisaglanz.com/) — Seamless digital paper texture packs
- [Creative Market](https://creativemarket.com/) — Thousands of texture packs from independent creators
- [Envato Elements](https://elements.envato.com/) — Subscription library with seasonal/organic/abstract textures

## 4. COLOR RESOURCES

**[Coolors](https://coolors.co/)** — Industry standard palette generator. Spacebar = infinite combinations. Lock colors you like. Palette from image, contrast checker, gradient generator. Free + Pro $5/mo.

**[Adobe Color](https://color.adobe.com/)** — Color wheel with harmony rules. AI extracts themes from images. Trending palettes from Behance/Adobe Stock. Free with Adobe account.

**[Realtime Colors](https://www.realtimecolors.com/)** — See palettes applied to real website layouts (hero, cards, buttons, links). Built-in contrast checker with AA/AAA. Free. Figma plugin.

**[Huemint](https://huemint.com/)** — ML-powered colors based on context. Shows how colors apply to brand identity, website, or illustration. Free + Pro $29/mo.

**[Happy Hues](https://www.happyhues.co/)** — Curated palettes demonstrated on live landing page. Click palette → entire site updates. Immediate context for how a palette feels. Free.

**[WebAIM Contrast Checker](https://webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker/)** — The standard accessibility tool. WCAG 2.0 AA (4.5:1) and AAA (7:1). Non-negotiable for professional work.

**[Khroma](https://www.khroma.co/)** — AI learns your color preferences, generates personalized palettes. Free.
**[Color Hunt](https://colorhunt.co/)** — Community-curated palette collections. Clean and fast.

## 5. DESIGN TOOLS AND PRODUCTION

**[Figma](https://www.figma.com/)** — Dominant design tool. Component systems, auto-layout, plugins (Iconify, LottieFiles, Photopea). AI-powered object removal and image extension (late 2025). Free tier for individuals.

**[Canva](https://www.canva.com/)** — Template-based design at scale. Brand Kit for consistency. Strengths: speed, accessibility, collaboration.

**[Photopea](https://www.photopea.com/)** — Free, browser-based Photoshop alternative. Supports PSD, AI, XD, Sketch, PDF. Layers, masks, blend modes, filters, smart objects. No install, no account.

**[Remove.bg](https://www.remove.bg/)** — AI background removal. Free for low-res; paid for high-res. API for batch processing.

**[Vectorizer.ai](https://vectorizer.ai/)** — Raster to clean vector (SVG) using AI. Production-ready vectors with proper curves.

## 6. DESIGN EDUCATION AND KNOWLEDGE

### Books and Courses
**[Refactoring UI](https://refactoringui.com/)** — By Adam Wathan + Steve Schoger (TailwindCSS creators). 218-page practical design guide. Covers spacing, typography, color, depth, hierarchy, layout. Widely considered the single most practical design resource.

**[Laws of UX](https://lawsofux.com/)** — Design principles visualized. Fitts's Law, Von Restorff Effect, Aesthetic-Usability Effect, 80/20 rule. Free reference + book.

### Publications
- **[Smashing Magazine](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/)** — Deep technical articles on design, CSS, UX, accessibility
- **[Creative Bloq](https://www.creativebloq.com/)** — Tutorials, tool reviews, inspiration across all design disciplines
- **[Creative Boom](https://www.creativeboom.com/)** — Independent foundries, design trends, creative industry news

### YouTube Channels

## 7. MOTION AND ANIMATION

**[LottieFiles](https://lottiefiles.com/)** — Standard animation library. JSON-based format, lightweight, cross-device. 280,000+ companies. Customizable to brand colors.

**Motion for Social Media:**
- Brand consistency: stick to brand colors and style
- Simplicity: don't overcrowd with text
- Purpose: animation should improve engagement, not just exist
- Natural motion: apply easing (ease-in, ease-out)
- Duration: under 15s for feed, under 5s for stories
