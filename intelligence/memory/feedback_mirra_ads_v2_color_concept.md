name: Mirra ads color variety and concept sharpness
description: Don't overuse pink/salmon in every ad. Vary color moods. Headlines must be identity-first and Mirra-specific, not generic meal delivery copy.
type: feedback
---

1. **Color variety** — Don't make every ad pink/salmon/dusty rose. Vary across batch:
   - Some: warm cream/linen dominant (subtle, editorial)
   - Some: charcoal/dark with cream text (contrast, premium)
   - Some: salmon/blush (but not all)
   - The pink should feel like an accent sometimes, not the whole palette

**Why:** When every ad is pink, they blur together in feed. Variety = scroll-stopping contrast between ads. Also, pink fatigue makes the brand feel one-note.

**How to apply:** In COLOR_MOOD prompt layer, don't hardcode "salmon/dusty rose" for all. Create 3-4 color mood variants and rotate across the batch. At most 40% of batch should lead with pink.

2. **Concept sharpness** — Headlines like "your monday to thursday, sorted" or "eat well without trying" are GENERIC. Any meal delivery brand can say that.

**Why:** Mirra's winners are identity-first: "Mirra's fault for loose jeans" = specific, funny, only-Mirra. The voice rules say "speak to who they are, not what the product does." Generic = invisible in feed.

**How to apply:** Every headline must pass the "could GrabFood say this?" test. If yes, rewrite. Winning patterns:
- Outcome-specific: "loose jeans", "coworker asked what diet"
- Price-shocking: "RM17.75 and she's still eating pad thai"
- Identity: "the girl who has her lunch figured out"
- Anti-pattern: "i stopped pretending sad desk salad is a personality"

3. **Food must look like Mirra's actual bento** — if the food photo doesn't render as recognizable Mirra bento in the final ad, it failed. The food should be clearly a bento box with compartments, not a generic bowl.
