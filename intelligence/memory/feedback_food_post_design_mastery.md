## FOOD POST DESIGN MASTERY — WHAT THE USER ACTUALLY WANTS

### THE GAP (March 30 2026)
User's Pinterest boards (mirra-photo-mood + typography-post-design) show a COMPLETELY different standard than what was produced. 11 food posts were rejected as "all same layout and font slapped on it. Zero effort."

### PHOTO MOOD DNA (from user's board)
1. **HUMAN HANDS/BODY in frame** — sprinkling microgreens, holding bowls, twirling pasta, reaching for dishes. Food is being INTERACTED with, not sitting on a background.
2. **REAL ENVIRONMENTS** — kitchen counters, office desks with headphones, cafe tables with rattan chairs, wooden spreads with linen. Food lives in a CONTEXT.
3. **NATURAL LIGHT** — side-angle window light, overhead with shadows, golden hour warmth. Not flat studio.
4. **PROPS + TEXTURE** — linen napkins, gold utensils, plants, iced tea glasses, olive oil bottles. The SCENE tells a story.
5. **CAMERA ANGLES** — side angle (45°), hero hold (hands from above), action shots (sprinkling, pouring). Not just top-down flat-lay.
6. **ABUNDANCE** — multiple dishes on table, spread layout, overflowing bowls. Food looks generous.

### TYPOGRAPHY DNA (from user's board)
1. **MASSIVE TYPE — 60-100% of canvas.** Not small labels. "CIAO!", "BOWL'D", "MUNCH!" = the type IS the design.
2. **FOOD INTEGRATED WITH TYPE** — pasta on fork IN FRONT of "CIAO!" text. Bread shaped AS a mountain under "REACH YOUR PEAK". Food and type are ONE composition, not separate layers.
3. **BOLD COLOR VARIETY** — yellow monochrome, electric blue, red, green, sage, cream. NOT just blush pink for everything.
4. **CONCEPTUAL DESIGN** — bread = mountain peak, toasts = brand archetypes, spoon = "NO SUGAR". Each post has a CONCEPT that's clever.
5. **MIXED TYPE WEIGHTS** — bold display + italic accent + small utility text + circled words + badges. Multiple type styles in one composition.
6. **GRAPHIC ELEMENTS** — ticket/label overlays, calorie badges, stickers, circled words, globe icons. Design DEVICES beyond just text.
7. **FOOD AS HERO OBJECT** — chips floating, pasta on fork, cream on spoon, bread as mountain. Food is dynamically composed, not sitting flat in a bento box.

### NEVER DO (for food posts)
- Same "WHAT'S INSIDE" layout on all labeled bentos
- Small serif labels in white boxes on blush pink (template spam)
- Flat bento on solid-color background with zero context
- Zero human presence (no hands, no body, no interaction)
- Zero props/environment (no linen, no utensils, no desk)
- Same font treatment across all posts
- Same color mood across all posts
- "Clean editorial feel" as the ONLY instruction (lazy)

### ALWAYS DO (for food posts)
- Each food post = its own UNIQUE design concept/world
- Massive typography as primary design element (60%+ canvas)
- Food integrated WITH the design (through type, behind type, AS concept)
- BOLD color choices that vary per post (not all pink)
- Human hands/body in frame where possible
- Props and environment context
- Conceptual design thinking (what clever idea makes THIS dish interesting?)
- Multiple type weights/sizes in one composition
- At least 3 visually DIFFERENT design approaches per 5 food posts

**Why:** User said "all same layout and font slapped on it. Zero effort." The Pinterest boards show editorial design mastery — each post should stop the scroll with a DESIGN IDEA, not just information.

**How to apply:** Before producing ANY food post, ask: "What is the DESIGN CONCEPT for this post? How is this different from the last one?" If the answer is "same layout, different dish" = REJECT before generating.
