## 1. THE 2026 META LANDSCAPE (ANDROMEDA ERA) <a name="andromeda"></a>

### The Andromeda Algorithm — Everything Changed
Meta's new **Andromeda retrieval algorithm**, powered by NVIDIA's GH200 Grace Hopper Superchip + Meta's in-house MTIA chip, fundamentally changed how ads work:

- **100x faster** at matching people to ads
- **10,000x more ad variants** processed in parallel
- Core philosophical shift: Instead of "Who should see this ad?" the system now asks **"Which ad should this specific person see right now?"**

### How Andromeda Analyzes Your Creative
Andromeda evaluates creatives across **5 dimensions simultaneously**:
1. **Visual structure** — format, pacing, framing, scene changes
2. **Narrative logic** — problem-solution, education, social proof, aspiration
3. **Hook mechanics** — first 1-3 seconds, pattern interruption, curiosity triggers
4. **Emotional tone** — authority, relatability, urgency, calm confidence
5. **Engagement behavior** — hold rate, rewatches, saves, comments, downstream clicks

### What This Means
- **Creative IS the new targeting.** Andromeda determines who sees your ads based on creative quality and type, not your audience settings
- **Targeting inputs are suggestions.** Meta treats your targeting as loose guidelines, not rules
- **Campaign simplicity = more signal density.** Fragmented structures dilute the data AI needs
- **Meta is no longer a media-buying platform — it's a creative discovery engine**

### Attribution Change (March 2026)
- **Engage-through attribution** replaced engage-view attribution
- Click-through attribution now requires an actual link click (not just any click)
- Engage-through has a **1-day window** (down from 7 days)
- **Remarketing hardest hit** — conversions 2-7 days post-engage no longer counted

**1. Consolidate Everything**
- Ideally ONE Advantage+ campaign
- Combine cold and warm traffic into unified ad sets

**2. Creative Diversity is a Survival Tactic**
Ways to engineer creative diversity:
- **Different ad formats**: static images, videos, partnership ads, carousels
- **Different angles**: the core reason your prospects should buy (each angle = different ad)

- Work with micro-influencers for authentic content
- Meta's algorithm advantage: shows ads to the creator's audience first

- Upload external data sources daily
- Use tracking tools to inform every decision
- Don't trust Meta's numbers alone

## 3. CHARLEY TICHENOR III <a name="charley-tichenor"></a>
**Background:** $500M+ in managed Facebook ad spend. Creator of the Facebook Ads MBA Program. "The Disrupter."

### Campaign Structure: The 1-Campaign Account

**The entire account runs ONE CBO campaign.**

| Element | Setting |
|---------|---------|
| Campaign type | CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization / Advantage+ Campaign Budget) |
| Targeting | **Broad only** — age, gender, location. Nothing else. |
| Audiences | No interests. No behaviors. No lookalikes. No custom audiences. |
| Philosophy | Let ads be "unobstructed with the audiences they create for themselves" |

### Why 1 Campaign Works
- Centralizes investment to the single best use of resources
- Facebook's ML/AI gets better with every impression
- Broad targeting = absence of inhibition on every ad's own lookalike audience
- Most stable, most scalable, most projectable option
- Starts as cheapest option AND gets smarter over time

### The 3:2:2 Creative Testing Method

**Structure per test:**
- **3 different creatives** (video or image) — these are the "hooks"
- **2 sets of copy** (primary text variations)
- **2 headlines**
- All with the **same underlying concept**

**Rules:**
- Use **dynamic creative testing** (NOT individual post IDs)
- Individual post IDs create internal competition, diminishing results
- Dynamic creative = each iteration is mutually beneficial
- Test ONE variable at a time within the 3:2:2 framework
- CTA text doesn't matter much — focus testing on creative elements

### Scaling Approach
- Scientific method applied to ads
- Let the meritocracy of delivery work
- Don't micromanage — Facebook's ML handles optimization
- Add new 3:2:2 tests continuously to feed the system

### Contrarian Insights
> "Lookalike audiences, interest groups, and retargeting don't work the way you think they do." Broad targeting outperforms all segmentation because it lets the algorithm find your buyers without restrictions.

> "You only need 1 campaign." Every additional campaign fragments your data and makes Facebook dumber.

## 4. ANDREW HUBBARD / SCALING CONSENSUS <a name="andrew-hubbard"></a>
**Background:** Scaling strategist. ROAS optimization specialist.

### The 2026 Scaling Consensus (Multiple Experts Agree)

**Two-Campaign Structure Wins**
- Testing campaign (20-30% budget) for new creatives
- Scaling campaign (70-80% budget) for proven winners
- This is the dominant structure across top advertisers in 2026

**CPMr: The Metric That Separates Scale from Stagnation**
- **CPMr** = Cost per 1,000 Reach
- Rising CPMr = paying to show same ads to same people (BAD)
- Healthy CPMr = **<$20** = creative is expanding reach to new qualified audiences
- Track CPMr weekly as your primary scaling health indicator

**Scaling Rules**
- Increase budget **10-20% at a time**
- Wait **several days** between adjustments
- Aggressive scaling disrupts learning phase and spikes costs
- Creative fatigue is the #1 reason for scaling failures

**Creative Scaling ≠ Volume**
- Creative scaling = systematic production of **diverse** ad creatives at velocity
- Meta's algorithm uses creative assets to determine which auctions you enter and what you pay
- Format diversity (static, video, carousel) + messaging angle diversity + systematic refresh cadence

### Budget Allocation Framework
| Segment | Budget % | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Advantage+ Shopping / Sales | 60-70% | Broad prospecting + retargeting |
| Manual Prospecting | 20-30% | Specific audiences + testing |
| Manual Retargeting | 10-20% | DPA + specific remarketing |

## 5. DEPESH MANDALIA <a name="depesh-mandalia"></a>
**Background:** One of the world's top bid strategy specialists. Multiple $26M+ scaling case studies. 21X ROAS case studies published.

### Bidding Strategy Mastery

**The 4 Bidding Strategies (Ranked by Risk/Reward)**

| Strategy | Analogy | Best For | Risk Level |
|----------|---------|----------|------------|
| **Cost Cap** | "Steady Eddie" — the Honda | Keeping average cost stable while Meta optimizes delivery | Low |
| **Minimum ROAS** | The accountant | Managing profitability directly | Low-Medium |
| **Bid Cap** | The surgeon's scalpel | Tapping into profitable audiences when account underperforms | Medium-High |
| **Accelerated Bids** | The Ferrari | Super fast scaling (Black Friday, flash events) | HIGH |

### The 4X CPA Band Testing Method

**How Depesh tests bid caps:**
1. Set target CPA (e.g., $10)
2. Create 4 ad sets with bid caps at **1x, 2x, 3x, 4x** target CPA ($10, $20, $30, $40)
3. Run all simultaneously
4. Best performance **usually falls around 3x CPA** ($30 in this example)
5. Hone down from there for optimal ROAS/CPA balance

### Case Study: Reducing Costs 50% While Scaling
- Goal: CPA of £9 or below
- Tested £6.75 bid cap: good CPA/ROAS but **couldn't spend** (too restrictive)
- Higher bids tested for comparison
- **£9 Cost Cap was the sweet spot** — best balance between spending enough and performing well

### Key Principles
- **Bid Cap is the ONLY true manual bid** — if you want hands-on control, use Bid Cap
- Bid Cap needs **far more daily maintenance** than other strategies
- Cost Cap aims for average cost stability; Bid Cap sets strict maximum per auction
- **Accelerated bids remove pacing** — budget can burn fast, not for inexperienced
- For fast-scaling events: use manual bid strategies. For steady growth: Cost Cap

### Contrarian Insights
> "Most advertisers are afraid of manual bidding. But it's the fastest and most reliable form of scaling Facebook ads." The ones who master bid caps have an unfair advantage.

> Testing at 4x your target CPA sounds insane, but the algorithm needs room to find the right users. Too-tight bids starve your campaigns.

## 6. ALEX HORMOZI <a name="alex-hormozi"></a>
**Background:** $100M+ business portfolio. Author of $100M Offers and $100M Leads. Acquisition.com founder.

### The 6-Angle x 5-Hook Ad Framework

**How to create 30-60 ads systematically:**
1. Develop **6 different angles** (the "meat" of each ad — different reasons to buy)
2. Bolt **5 different hooks** onto each angle = **30 unique ads**
3. Film each ad multiple times = **60 ads**
4. Run each in its own ad set to force Meta to spend on each

### The 5 Hook Categories
| Category | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Far Past** | Important life lessons | "3 years ago I weighed 90kg..." |
| **Recent Past** | What happened recently | "A client just told me..." |
| **Present** | Ideas captured in real-time | "I'm literally eating this right now..." |
| **Trending** | Apply expertise to current events | "Everyone's talking about X, but..." |
| **Manufactured** | Create new experiences to document | "I tried eating only X for 30 days..." |

### Headlines Should Use "The News" Elements
- Recency, Relevancy, Celebrity, Proximity, Conflict, Unusual, Ongoing

### Hook-Retain-Reward System
| Phase | Purpose | Execution |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **HOOK** | Grab attention | Bold, compelling headline/visual. First 0.5-3 seconds. |
| **RETAIN** | Keep engaged | Storytelling or valuable content |
| **REWARD** | Drive action | Actionable insight or compelling offer → next step |

### The "More — Better — New" Scaling Framework
From $100M Leads:

1. **MORE** — Do more of what's already working. Add more creatives, more spend.
2. **BETTER** — Optimize through testing. "You do more and more until it breaks. Then you make it better." Better gives more results for same effort.
3. **NEW** — Only after "better" yields diminishing returns. Try new placements, new formats, new channels.

**When to move between stages:** "When the returns you get from doing more/better are lower than what you could get from a new placement or new way of advertising."

### Lead Magnet Philosophy
> "Give until they ask." Flood your audience with so much value that they can't help but reach out. The best lead magnets solve a narrow problem upfront while naturally leading to your higher-ticket offer.

### Contrarian Insight
> "The offer is the ad." Most advertisers optimize creative when they should be optimizing the offer. A great offer with a mediocre ad beats a mediocre offer with a great ad, every time.

## 10. BARRY HOTT <a name="barry-hott"></a>
**Background:** $600M+ in managed ad spend. 16+ years in performance marketing. Worked with Toyota, Kraft, Spotify. Known as "The Ugly Ads Guy."

### The "Ugly Ads" Philosophy

> "If your marketing is too pretty, it's probably not going to work on paid social."

**What "ugly" means:**
- Camera can be shaky
- Lighting can be natural/sun
- Audio can be imperfect
- Video can be low resolution
- Memes, news-style content, native-feeling content
- NOT polished brand content
- NOT standard UGC either — more like content users WANT to see on the platform

**Goal:** Meet consumers where they are, in the content style they expect to see. Put branding aside so the ad feels relatable.

### Performance Results (Ugly vs. Pretty)
- Lone Ranch Water test: ~10 ads (pretty vs. ugly). **Ugly ads won.**
- After months of running: Facebook brand lift study showed **~30% lift in action intent**
- Simple ugly ads outperform polished content with:
  - **~3x the click rate**
  - **~3-5x higher conversion rate**

### The 90/10 Rule

**90% net new creative. 10% iteration.**

This solves "Iteration Paralysis" — when marketing teams keep recycling the same winning formulas until they stop winning.

How Iteration Paralysis happens:
1. New creative strategists get trapped in data-driven loops
2. Every campaign becomes a slight variation of the last win
3. Messaging gets repetitive
4. Audiences tune out
5. Performance slowly dies

**Solution:** Champion vs. Challenger testing. Always try to beat your current "best" performer with something genuinely new.

### 2026 Advantage
Andromeda can handle **10,000x more ad variants in parallel** — the system REWARDS volume of genuinely different creatives. Barry's ugly ads approach = perfect match for Andromeda's creative diversity appetite.

### Contrarian Insights
> "Your beautiful brand guidelines are killing your ad performance." The ads that look worst in a brand deck perform best in the feed.

> "90% of your creative should be NET NEW — not iterations." Most advertisers do the opposite (90% iteration, 10% new).

## 12. UNIVERSAL CONSENSUS: What ALL Experts Agree On <a name="consensus"></a>

### 1. Creative > Targeting (Unanimous)
Every single expert agrees: in 2026, creative quality and diversity is MORE important than audience targeting. Andromeda's creative-first matching means your ads' visual/narrative quality determines distribution.

### 2. Simplify Campaign Structure (Unanimous)
All experts recommend fewer campaigns. Range from 1 (Charley) to 2 (Ben Heath, Andrew Hubbard). Nobody recommends 5+ campaigns.

### 3. Broad Targeting Wins (Strong Consensus)
Most experts recommend broad targeting (age, gender, location only) or letting Advantage+ handle targeting. Interest-based and behavioral targeting is dying.

### 4. Test Volume of DIVERSE Creatives (Unanimous)
Not color variations. Genuinely different angles, hooks, formats, and messaging. The system rewards diversity, not volume of similar ads.

### 5. Hook in First 0.5-3 Seconds (Unanimous)
90% of users stop scrolling before 3 seconds. The hook is the most important element. Multiple experts dedicate entire frameworks to hooks alone.

### 6. UGC/Native > Polished (Strong Consensus)
Ugly, authentic, native-feeling content outperforms polished brand content. Barry Hott's data: 3x CTR, 3-5x conversion rate.

### 7. Data-Driven Creative Decisions (Unanimous)
Every expert emphasizes using analytics (Motion, Meta reporting, external tools) to inform creative strategy — not gut feeling.

## 15. ACTION PLAYBOOK: Synthesized System <a name="playbook"></a>

### Step 1: Account Structure
- **Option A (Conservative):** 2 campaigns — Scaling (80%) + Testing (20%)
- **Option B (Advanced):** 1 CBO campaign, broad targeting only (Charley method)
- Both: Advantage+ Campaign Budget. Broad targeting (age, gender, location).

### Step 2: Bidding
- **Default:** Lowest Cost (let Meta optimize)
- **Scaling events:** Cost Cap at target CPA
- **Fast scaling:** Bid Cap tested at 1x/2x/3x/4x CPA bands (sweet spot ~3x)
- **Flash events (BFCM etc.):** Accelerated bids (expert only)

### Step 3: Creative Production System
1. **Research first** (Nick Theriot): Reddit mining, customer language, persona mapping
2. **Build angles** (Hormozi): 6 angles x 5 hooks = 30 ads minimum
3. **Match to awareness** (Nick Theriot): Unaware → Most Aware ads for each persona
4. **Produce ugly** (Barry Hott): Native, authentic, platform-native content. 90% new.
5. **Use creators** (Ben Heath / Savannah): Don't over-script. Hook + 3 talking points.
6. **Test with 3:2:2** (Charley): 3 creatives, 2 copy, 2 headlines per concept.
7. **Analyze weekly** (Dara Denney): Data download → winning patterns → new briefs.

### Step 4: Creative Diversity Checklist
- [ ] Multiple ad formats (static, video, carousel, partnership)
- [ ] Multiple angles (different reasons to buy)
- [ ] Multiple hooks (different opening lines/scenes)
- [ ] Multiple awareness levels (unaware through most aware)
- [ ] Multiple personas (different customer types)
- [ ] Multiple tones (urgent, calm, funny, authoritative)
- [ ] Ugly/native versions alongside any polished content

### Step 5: Scaling Protocol
1. Monitor CPMr weekly — must stay <$20
2. Scale budget 10-20% at a time, wait several days
3. When frequency >4.0 or CTR drops >20% → refresh creative
4. Refresh every 2-3 weeks regardless
5. 20-30% budget always allocated to testing new creative
6. "More → Better → New" (Hormozi): exhaust current approach before trying new

### Step 6: Measurement
- Set up proper attribution (engage-through changes)
- Use Value Rules to tell Meta which conversions matter
- Upload external conversion data
- Don't optimize for top-of-funnel metrics (clicks, views) — optimize for purchases/leads
- Performance Goal is the most important setting in your campaign
